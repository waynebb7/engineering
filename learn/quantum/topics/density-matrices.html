1) Why density matrices?

Not every quantum system is in a definite pure state \(|\psi\rangle\). Thermal ensembles, uncontrolled interactions, and ignorance of preparation details require a mixed state — a statistical mixture of pure states described by a density operator \(\rho\).

Pure vs mixed: Pure \(\rho = |\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\) has \(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho^2)=1\); mixed states have \(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho^2) < 1\). Measurement of mixed ensembles: Superposition and Measurement.

2) Definition and axioms

A density operator \(\rho\) on Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\) satisfies:

  • Hermitian: \(\rho = \rho^\dagger\)
  • Positive semidefinite: \(\langle\phi|\rho|\phi\rangle \geq 0\) for all \(|\phi\rangle\)
  • Normalised: \(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho) = 1\)

Expectation value of observable \(A\): \(\langle A \rangle = \mathrm{Tr}(\rho A)\).

Bra–ket machinery: Dirac Notation; postulate extension: Postulates of Quantum Mechanics.

3) Ensemble interpretation

If a system is prepared in state \(|\psi_i\rangle\) with probability \(p_i\) (\(\sum_i p_i = 1\)):

\[\rho = \sum_i p_i\,|\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i|\]

Off-diagonal elements (coherences) in a given basis encode superposition within ensemble members; diagonal elements encode classical probabilities in that basis.

Maximally mixed qubit: \(\rho = I/2\) — complete ignorance; \(\langle\sigma_i\rangle = 0\) for all Pauli directions.

4) Purity and von Neumann entropy

Purity: \(\gamma = \mathrm{Tr}(\rho^2)\). For qubits, \(0.5 \leq \gamma \leq 1\).

Von Neumann entropy: \(S(\rho) = -\mathrm{Tr}(\rho\log_2\rho)\) bits (or nats with \(\ln\)). Pure states have \(S=0\); maximally mixed single qubit has \(S=1\) bit.

Spectral form

\(\rho = \sum_k \lambda_k |k\rangle\langle k|\) with eigenvalues \(\lambda_k \geq 0\), \(\sum \lambda_k = 1\).

Entropy

\(S = -\sum_k \lambda_k \log_2 \lambda_k\) — Shannon entropy of eigenvalue distribution.

5) Reduced states and partial trace

For composite system \(AB\) in state \(\rho_{AB}\), the reduced state of subsystem \(A\) is:

\[\rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB})\]

Tracing out an unobserved partner generically produces a mixed state even when \(\rho_{AB}\) is pure — the mathematical signature of Entanglement.

Example: For \(|\Phi^+\rangle = (|00\rangle+|11\rangle)/\sqrt{2}\), \(\rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(|\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|) = I/2\) — maximally mixed marginal.

6) Bloch vector picture (qubit)

Any qubit \(\rho\) can be written \(\rho = \dfrac{1}{2}(I + \mathbf{r}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma})\) where \(|\mathbf{r}| \leq 1\). Pure states lie on the Bloch sphere surface (\(|\mathbf{r}|=1\)); mixed states lie inside. See Spin and Pauli Matrices.

7) Quantum operations on \(\rho\)

Unitary evolution: \(\rho \to U\rho U^\dagger\). General open-system evolution (noise, decoherence) is described by completely positive trace-preserving (CPTP) maps — see Quantum Channels.

  • Depolarising channel: \(\rho \to (1-p)\rho + p\,I/d\)
  • Amplitude damping: energy relaxation to ground state
  • Phase damping: loss of coherences without energy change
