1) Kets and bras

A quantum state is a vector in a Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\). Dirac notation writes:

  • Ket \(|\psi\rangle\) — column vector (state)
  • Bra \(\langle\phi|\) — row vector (dual vector)
  • Inner product: \(\langle\phi|\psi\rangle\) — complex scalar
Convention: \(\langle\phi|\psi\rangle\) is linear in \(|\psi\rangle\) and anti-linear in \(\langle\phi|\). Normalisation: \(\langle\psi|\psi\rangle = 1\).

Hilbert-space foundations: Hilbert Spaces.

2) Outer products and projectors

The outer product \(|a\rangle\langle b|\) is an operator. A rank-one projector onto normalised \(|a\rangle\) is:

\[P_a = |a\rangle\langle a|, \quad P_a^2 = P_a, \quad P_a^\dagger = P_a\]

Completeness of an orthonormal basis \(\{|n\rangle\}\): \(\displaystyle\sum_n |n\rangle\langle n| = I\).

Used in Superposition and Measurement for the projection postulate.

3) Operators and matrix elements

A linear operator \(A\) acts on kets: \(A|\psi\rangle = |\phi\rangle\). Matrix element:

\[A_{mn} = \langle m | A | n \rangle\]

Hermitian operators (\(A = A^\dagger\)) represent observables; unitary operators (\(U^\dagger U = I\)) represent reversible evolution and basis changes.

Expectation value

\(\langle A \rangle = \langle\psi|A|\psi\rangle\)

Adjoint

\(\langle\phi|A|\psi\rangle^* = \langle\psi|A^\dagger|\phi\rangle\)

4) Position and momentum kets

Continuous bases use Dirac delta normalisation:

  • Position: \(|x\rangle\), \(\langle x|x'\rangle = \delta(x-x')\)
  • Momentum: \(|p\rangle\), \(\langle p|p'\rangle = \delta(p-p')\)
  • Overlap: \(\langle x|p\rangle = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}} e^{ipx/\hbar}\)
Wave function: \(\psi(x) = \langle x|\psi\rangle\) links abstract kets to Wave–Particle Duality probability amplitudes.

5) Tensor products for composite systems

For two subsystems \(A\) and \(B\), the joint Hilbert space is \(\mathcal{H}_{AB} = \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B\). Product states: \(|\psi\rangle_A \otimes |\phi\rangle_B\), written \(|\psi\rangle_A|\phi\rangle_B\) or \(|psi,\phi\rangle\).

Entangled states cannot be written as a single product — see Entanglement and Bell Inequalities.

6) Spin-\(\tfrac{1}{2}\) worked example

Computational basis for a qubit:

\[|0\rangle = \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}, \quad |1\rangle = \begin{pmatrix}0\\1\end{pmatrix}\]

General state: \(|\psi\rangle = \alpha|0\rangle + \beta|1\rangle\) with \(|\alpha|^2+|\beta|^2=1\). Pauli operators in this basis — Spin and Pauli Matrices.

Worked: \(\langle 0|\psi\rangle = \alpha\), so \(P(0) = |\alpha|^2\) (Born rule).

7) Why Dirac notation matters

  • Compact — no explicit integrals or matrices unless needed
  • Basis-independent — states and operators without committing to coordinates
  • Standard in quantum information, field theory, and computing literature
  • Direct path to Density Matrices \(\rho = \sum_i p_i|\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i|\)
