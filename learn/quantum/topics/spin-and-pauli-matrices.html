1) What is spin?

Spin is intrinsic angular momentum carried by particles — not literal spinning motion. Electrons, protons, and qubits are spin-\(\tfrac{1}{2}\): measurement along any axis yields \(+\hbar/2\) or \(-\hbar/2\) only.

Qubit link: A spin-\(\tfrac{1}{2}\) degree of freedom is the canonical two-level quantum system underlying quantum computing. States use Dirac Notation: \(|\psi\rangle = \alpha|0\rangle + \beta|1\rangle\).

2) Pauli matrices

The three Pauli matrices (working in the \(S_z\) eigenbasis):

\[\sigma_x = \begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix},\quad \sigma_y = \begin{pmatrix}0&-i\i&0\end{pmatrix},\quad \sigma_z = \begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix}\]

Spin operators: \(S_i = \dfrac{\hbar}{2}\sigma_i\) for \(i \in \{x,y,z\}\).

Relation Formula
Anticommutator \(\{\sigma_i,\sigma_j\} = 2\delta_{ij}I\)
Commutator \([\sigma_i,\sigma_j] = 2i\epsilon_{ijk}\sigma_k\)
Eigenvalues of \(\sigma_i\) \(\pm 1\)

3) Eigenstates and the Bloch sphere

\(S_z\) eigenstates (often labelled \(|0\rangle, |1\rangle\) or \(|\uparrow\rangle, |\downarrow\rangle\)):

\[|{\uparrow z}\rangle = |0\rangle = \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix},\quad |{\downarrow z}\rangle = |1\rangle = \begin{pmatrix}0\\1\end{pmatrix}\]

\(S_x\) eigenstates: \(|{\pm x}\rangle = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|0\rangle \pm |1\rangle)\). Any pure qubit state maps to a point on the Bloch sphere via \(\mathbf{r} = \langle\sigma_x\rangle\hat{x} + \langle\sigma_y\rangle\hat{y} + \langle\sigma_z\rangle\hat{z}\).

4) Measurement and non-commutativity

\([S_x, S_z] \neq 0\): measuring \(S_z\) then \(S_x\) gives different statistics than the reverse order. This is the spin manifestation of the Uncertainty Principle.

Worked: State \(|{\uparrow z}\rangle\): \(P(S_z = +\hbar/2) = 1\). Re-measure \(S_x\): outcomes \(\pm\hbar/2\) each with probability \(\tfrac{1}{2}\).

Measurement rules: Superposition and Measurement.

5) Spinors and rotations

Rotations about axis \(\hat{n}\) by angle \(\theta\) are generated by \(U(\hat{n},\theta) = e^{-i\theta\,\hat{n}\cdot\mathbf{S}/\hbar}\). A \(2\pi\) rotation picks up a factor of \(-1\) on spinors — only \(4\pi\) returns to \(+1\), verified in neutron interferometry.

Unitary evolution follows Postulate IV in Postulates of Quantum Mechanics.

6) Two-spin systems and entanglement

Joint Hilbert space dimension 4. The singlet state:

\[|\Psi^-\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|01\rangle - |10\rangle)\]

has total spin zero and perfect anti-correlations — a building block for Entanglement and Bell Inequalities.

7) Physical contexts

  • Stern–Gerlach: spatial separation of spin components by inhomogeneous magnetic fields
  • NMR / MRI: nuclear spin resonance manipulated with RF pulses
  • Electron spin resonance (ESR): solid-state and chemistry applications
  • Quantum gates: \(X,Y,Z,H\) gates are Pauli/unitary combinations on qubits
