1) The variational principle

For a Hamiltonian \(H\) with ground-state energy \(E_0\), any normalised trial state \(|\psi(\boldsymbol{\theta})\rangle\) satisfies the variational principle:

\[ E(\boldsymbol{\theta}) = \langle \psi(\boldsymbol{\theta}) | H | \psi(\boldsymbol{\theta}) \rangle \geq E_0 \]

The variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) chooses a parametrized quantum circuit (ansatz) to prepare \(|\psi(\boldsymbol{\theta})\rangle\), measures \(E(\boldsymbol{\theta})\) on hardware, and uses a classical optimiser to minimise \(E\) toward \(E_0\).

Key idea: Quantum device prepares states and estimates expectations; classical computer updates parameters — ideal for shallow circuits on NISQ devices.

Builds on Quantum Circuits and Measurement in Quantum Computing.

2) Hamiltonians as Pauli sums

Chemistry and spin models map to qubit Hamiltonians as weighted sums of Pauli strings:

\[ H = \sum_k c_k P_k, \qquad P_k \in \{I, X, Y, Z\}^{\otimes n} \]

The expectation \(\langle H \rangle = \sum_k c_k \langle P_k \rangle\) is estimated by measuring each term (or grouped commuting terms) in separate circuit layouts. Coefficients \(c_k\) come from fermion-to-qubit mappings (e.g. Jordan–Wigner, Bravyi–Kitaev).

3) The VQE loop

  1. Initialise parameters \(\boldsymbol{\theta}\) (random or warm-start)
  2. Prepare \(|\psi(\boldsymbol{\theta})\rangle = U(\boldsymbol{\theta})|0\rangle^{\otimes n}\) on quantum processor
  3. Measure expectations \(\langle P_k \rangle\) via basis rotations and shots
  4. Compute \(E(\boldsymbol{\theta}) = \sum_k c_k \langle P_k \rangle\) classically
  5. Update \(\boldsymbol{\theta}\) with optimiser (COBYLA, SPSA, gradient-based methods)
  6. Repeat until convergence
Shot noise: Finite shots give noisy \(E(\boldsymbol{\theta})\); optimisers must be robust (derivative-free methods are common on real hardware).

4) Ansätze: UCC and hardware-efficient

The ansatz \(U(\boldsymbol{\theta})\) constrains which states are explored:

  • Unitary coupled cluster (UCC): physics-motivated excitations; good for molecules but deep
  • Hardware-efficient: layers of \(R_y, R_z\) and entanglers matching native connectivity
  • ADAPT-VQE: grows the ansatz iteratively from operator pool
Barren plateaus: Random deep ansätze can have exponentially vanishing gradients in \(n\), making optimisation intractable — ansatz design matters.

Gate primitives: Quantum Gates. Compare combinatorial focus of QAOA.

5) Chemistry and materials applications

VQE targets the electronic Schrödinger equation for fixed nuclei. The computed ground energy \(E(\boldsymbol{\theta})\) approximates the true \(E_0\); bond dissociation curves and reaction profiles use repeated VQE runs.

  • H\(_2\), LiH, BeH\(_2\): canonical benchmark molecules
  • Active spaces: reduce qubit count by focusing on valence orbitals
  • Classical baselines: compare to coupled cluster CCSD(T) for validation
Limit: VQE finds an upper bound on \(E_0\) (variational); accuracy depends on ansatz expressiveness and noise, not on polynomial-time guarantees like Shor's algorithm.

6) Noise, mitigation, and NISQ practice

On noisy hardware, measured \(E(\boldsymbol{\theta})\) is biased. Practical workflows use:

  • Zero-noise extrapolation (ZNE): run at scaled noise and extrapolate to zero
  • Symmetry verification: discard shots violating particle-number or spin constraints
  • Measurement grouping: reduce circuit repetitions for Pauli terms
  • Error mitigation (not full correction): see Quantum Error Correction

7) Worked sketch: 2-qubit Heisenberg dimer

Hamiltonian \(H = J(\sigma_1^x \sigma_2^x + \sigma_1^y \sigma_2^y + \sigma_1^z \sigma_2^z)\) on two qubits. Ansatz: \(U(\theta) = e^{-i\theta X_1 Y_2}\) (or a short hardware-efficient layer).

Measure \(\langle H \rangle\) for several \(\theta\); classical optimiser finds minimum near the true ground energy. For \(J > 0\) antiferromagnetic Heisenberg, the singlet-like state minimises energy.

Exercise: Show \(\langle 00|H|00\rangle = J\) and compare to the true ground energy for your chosen \(J\) — the variational state must be expressive enough to beat the product state.
