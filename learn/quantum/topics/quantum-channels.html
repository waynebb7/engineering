1) Open quantum systems

Real qubits and quantum devices are not perfectly isolated. Interaction with baths, control electronics, and stray fields causes dissipation and decoherence. The state of the system alone is then described by a dynamical map on density matrices, not a unitary on pure kets.

State object: Channels act on Density Matrices \(\rho\), not only on \(|\psi\rangle\).

2) CPTP maps

A quantum channel \(\mathcal{E}\) is a linear map satisfying:

  • Trace preserving: \(\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal{E}(\rho)) = \mathrm{Tr}(\rho)\)
  • Completely positive (CP): \(\mathcal{E} \otimes I\) is positive for any extension

CP ensures physicality when the system is part of a larger composite — a cornerstone of modern quantum information theory.

Axioms background: Postulates of Quantum Mechanics (unitary limit).

3) Kraus operator-sum representation

Every CPTP map admits Kraus operators \(\{E_k\}\) such that:

\[\mathcal{E}(\rho) = \sum_k E_k \rho E_k^\dagger, \quad \sum_k E_k^\dagger E_k = I\]

Kraus operators model discrete quantum jumps (photon loss, spin flip, measurement back-action). Different Kraus sets can describe the same channel (operator gauge freedom).

5) Decoherence mechanism

Decoherence suppresses off-diagonal elements of \(\rho\) in a preferred basis (often energy or position), destroying interference and superposition visibility without necessarily changing populations. Environment entanglement implements a channel on the system — see Entanglement and Bell Inequalities.

Time scale: \(T_2\) (coherence time) ≤ \(T_1\) (energy relaxation). Quantum error correction fights channel-induced errors.

6) Stinespring dilation

Any channel \(\mathcal{E}\) on system \(S\) can be realised as unitary evolution on \(S \otimes E\) (environment) followed by tracing out \(E\):

\[\mathcal{E}(\rho) = \mathrm{Tr}_E\!\left[U(\rho \otimes |0\rangle\langle 0|_E)U^\dagger\right]\]

This unifies open-system dynamics with the unitary postulate on an enlarged Hilbert space — the mathematical basis for quantum circuit noise simulators.

7) Engineering: calibration and mitigation

  • Process tomography: reconstruct \(\mathcal{E}\) from measurement data
  • Dynamical decoupling: pulse sequences average out slow bath coupling
  • Zero-noise extrapolation: run at amplified noise, extrapolate to zero
  • Quantum error correction: encode logical qubits resilient to channel errors
  • Master equations: Lindblad form for Markovian open dynamics
