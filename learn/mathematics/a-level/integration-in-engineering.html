1) Integration as accumulation

Integration answers: how much has built up over time or space? If \( v = ds/dt \), then displacement is \( s = \int v\,dt \). If \( p = dE/dt \), energy is \( E = \int p\,dt \).

Key idea: Differentiation finds rates; integration finds totals. They are inverse operations and appear together throughout engineering analysis.

Prerequisite: Integration. Related: Differentiation in Engineering.

2) Electrical engineering — capacitors and inductors

Reactive components integrate current and voltage over time:

\[ v_C(t) = \frac{1}{C}\int i_C\,dt + v_0, \qquad i_L(t) = \frac{1}{L}\int v_L\,dt + i_0 \]

Capacitor

Charge accumulates: \( q = \int i\,dt \), and \( q = Cv \).

RC charging: \( v_C = V_s(1 - e^{-t/RC}) \) — integral of current through resistor.

Inductor

Current builds up gradually when voltage is applied.

RL circuit: \( i_L = \dfrac{V_s}{R}(1 - e^{-tR/L}) \).

Stored energy: \[ E_C = \frac{1}{2}CV^2, \qquad E_L = \frac{1}{2}LI^2 \] These follow from integrating power \( p = vi \) during charging.

3) Electrical engineering — energy, power, and signal processing

  • Energy from power: \( E = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} p(t)\,dt \) — basis of electricity metering (kWh)
  • Average power: \( \bar{P} = \dfrac{1}{T}\int_0^T v(t)i(t)\,dt \) — see Power Calculations
  • Op-amp integrator: \( v_{\text{out}} = -\dfrac{1}{RC}\int v_{\text{in}}\,dt \) — low-pass filtering and waveform shaping
  • PID integral term: \( K_i\int e(t)\,dt \) — eliminates steady-state error in control loops
Worked example: A 100 W heater runs for 3 hours. Energy consumed: \( E = Pt = 100 \times 3 \times 3600 = 1.08 \times 10^6 \) J = 0.3 kWh. For varying power, integrate: \( E = \int_0^T p(t)\,dt \).

4) Electrical engineering — electromagnetics

Field theory uses surface and volume integrals (preview for further study):

\[ \oint_S \vec{D}\cdot d\vec{A} = Q_{\text{enclosed}} \quad \text{(Gauss's law)} \] \[ \oint_C \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \quad \text{(Faraday's law)} \]

At A-Level, focus on the physical meaning: integrating a field over an area gives total flux or enclosed charge; integrating along a path gives induced EMF.

Scope note: Vector calculus integrals are covered in depth at university. Here, recognise that integration links local field quantities to global circuit effects (e.g. transformer induction).

5) Aircraft engineering — flight dynamics and navigation

Integration converts between acceleration, velocity, and position — the core of inertial navigation:

\[ v(t) = \int a(t)\,dt + v_0, \qquad s(t) = \int v(t)\,dt + s_0 \]

Worked example: An aircraft accelerates at \( a = 2 \) m s⁻² from rest. After 10 s: \( v = \int_0^{10} 2\,dt = 20 \) m s⁻¹. Distance: \( s = \int_0^{10} 2t\,dt = \left[t^2\right]_0^{10} = 100 \) m.
  • INS (Inertial Navigation System): integrates accelerometer data to track position — drift requires periodic correction
  • Fuel consumption: \( F = \int_0^T \dot{F}(t)\,dt \) — total fuel used over a flight segment
  • Flight simulators: numerical integration (Runge-Kutta) solves equations of motion step by step

6) Aircraft engineering — aerodynamics and structures

Aerodynamic forces

Total lift from surface pressure distribution:

\[ L = \int_A p(x,y)\,dA \]

Pressure varies across the wing; integration sums the contribution of each area element.

Structural deflection

Beam deflection from bending moment:

\[ y(x) = \iint \frac{M(x)}{EI}\,dx^2 \]

Double integration of moment gives displacement — critical for wing and fuselage design.

Propulsion: Mass flow rate through an engine inlet: \( \dot{m} = \int_A \rho v\,dA \), where \( \rho \) is air density and \( v \) is local velocity. Thrust depends on the rate of momentum change of this flow.

7) Numerical integration and simulation

Most engineering integrals are evaluated numerically when analytical solutions are unavailable:

  • Trapezium rule / Simpson's rule — A-Level numerical methods for definite integrals
  • Euler method: \( y_{n+1} = y_n + f(t_n)\,\Delta t \) — basic ODE stepping
  • Runge-Kutta methods — higher accuracy for flight simulation and circuit simulators (SPICE)
  • Embedded systems: accumulate sensor readings: position += velocity × Δt
Electrical–mechanical analogy: The same integration techniques solve RLC circuits and mass-spring-damper systems. Simulation tools solve both using identical numerical integrators.
