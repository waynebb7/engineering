1) What is division?

Division splits a total into equal parts or finds how many equal groups fit into a total.

  • Written with the division sign: ÷
  • 12 ÷ 3 = 4 can mean “12 shared equally among 3” or “how many 3s in 12?”
  • Division is the inverse of multiplication
Words for division: divide, share, split equally, grouped into, quotient.

2) Sharing and grouping

Sharing (partitive)

18 sweets shared among 6 children → each gets 18 ÷ 6 = 3 sweets.

Grouping (quotitive)

How many teams of 5 from 30 players? → 30 ÷ 5 = 6 teams.

Same calculation, different story: Both situations use division even though the question feels different.

3) Division and times tables

Knowing multiplication facts helps you divide quickly.

Example: If 8 × 7 = 56, then 56 ÷ 8 = 7 and 56 ÷ 7 = 8.
Common mistake: Dividing in the wrong order — 8 ÷ 56 is not the same as 56 ÷ 8.

4) Remainders

Sometimes division does not come out exactly. The remainder is what is left over.

Worked example: 17 ÷ 5 = 3 remainder 2 (written 3 r 2)
Check: (3 × 5) + 2 = 17

In word problems, decide whether to round up, round down, or give a remainder — depends on context (e.g. boxes needed vs people left over).

5) Bus stop method (short division)

Divide digit by digit from left to right, carrying remainders to the next column.

Worked example: 156 ÷ 6
1 ÷ 6 → 0, carry 1
15 ÷ 6 → 2, remainder 3
36 ÷ 6 → 6 → answer 26

6) Long division (KS3)

For dividing by larger numbers, use long division: estimate, multiply, subtract, bring down the next digit.

Steps

  1. How many times does the divisor fit?
  2. Multiply and subtract
  3. Bring down the next digit
  4. Repeat until done

Check

Multiply quotient × divisor (+ remainder if any) to verify.

7) Word problems

Clue words: shared equally, each, per, split, how many groups.

Worked example: 96 chairs arranged in rows of 8. How many rows?
96 ÷ 8 = 12 rows.
