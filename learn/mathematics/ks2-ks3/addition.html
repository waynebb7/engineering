1) What is addition?

Addition combines two or more amounts to find a total (the sum).

  • We write it with the plus sign: +
  • Example: 4 + 3 = 7 means 4 combined with 3 makes 7
  • The numbers being added are called addends
Words for addition: plus, add, sum, total, altogether, combined, increase.

2) Addition as combining groups

You can model addition by putting groups together:

  • 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples
  • On a number line: start at 3, jump forward 2 → land on 5

Counting on strategy

For 8 + 5, start at 8 and count on 5: 9, 10, 11, 12, 13.

Make ten strategy

Partition one number: 8 + 5 = 8 + 2 + 3 = 10 + 3 = 13.

3) Number bonds

Number bonds are pairs of numbers that add to a fixed total.

  • Bonds to 10: 1+9, 2+8, 3+7, 4+6, 5+5
  • Bonds to 20, 100, and 1 follow the same idea
Why they matter: Knowing bonds to 10 makes mental maths and column addition much faster.
Common mistake: Forgetting to add a carried digit in column addition. Always include the carry in the next column.

4) Properties of addition

Commutative

Order does not matter: 3 + 5 = 5 + 3.

Associative

Grouping does not matter: (2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4).

  • Identity: adding zero changes nothing — 7 + 0 = 7
KS3 note: These properties justify rearranging numbers in longer calculations to make mental addition easier.

5) Column addition (written method)

Line up numbers by place value (units under units, tens under tens).

  1. Add the units column first
  2. If the total is 10 or more, carry 1 to the next column
  3. Repeat for tens, hundreds, and so on
Worked example: 47 + 38
Units: 7 + 8 = 15 → write 5, carry 1
Tens: 4 + 3 + 1 = 8 → answer 85

6) Adding decimals and money

When adding decimals, line up the decimal points, not just the last digits.

Example: 3.6 + 2.45 = 6.05
Write as 3.60 + 2.45 so both have two decimal places.
Common mistake: Treating 3.6 + 2.45 like whole numbers and getting 5.105. Always align decimal points.

7) Word problems

Look for clue words: altogether, total, combined, in all, sum.

Worked example: A shop has 24 red pens and 17 blue pens. How many pens altogether?
24 + 17 = 41 pens.

Always check: does your answer make sense? 41 is reasonable for two groups in the twenties.
