1) The Clay Millennium Prize

In 2000, the Clay Mathematics Institute (CMI) announced seven prize problems, each carrying a \$1,000,000 bounty for a correct solution published in a refereed journal and verified by the mathematical community over two years.

The problems were chosen to represent deep, historically significant questions across pure mathematics and mathematical physics. They are intended to focus attention on frontier research, not merely to reward speed.

Prize rules (summary): A solution must be published in a major refereed journal, accepted by the community, and stand unrefuted for two years after publication. CMI's Scientific Advisory Board makes the final award determination.

3) P vs NP

Does every problem whose positive instances can be verified in polynomial time also admit a polynomial-time solution? \(\mathrm{P} = \mathrm{NP}\) would collapse thousands of hard optimization and reasoning problems into tractable ones; \(\mathrm{P} \neq \mathrm{NP}\) is the prevailing expectation.

Central result: Cook–Levin theorem: SAT is NP-complete. A polynomial algorithm for any NP-complete problem resolves the question.

Full coverage: P vs NP — Research guide.

4) Hodge Conjecture

On a smooth projective variety \(X\) over \(\mathbb{C}\), every Hodge class in \(H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Q}) \cap H^{p,p}(X)\) should be a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-linear combination of classes of algebraic cycles. This bridges transcendental Hodge theory and algebraic geometry.

Known case: Lefschetz (1,1) theorem proves the conjecture for \(p = 1\) (divisors). Higher codimension remains open.

Full coverage: Hodge Conjecture — Research guide.

5) Poincaré Conjecture (solved)

Every simply connected, closed 3-manifold is homeomorphic to the 3-sphere \(S^3\). Perelman proved this via Ricci flow with surgery, building on Hamilton's program. The proof also established the Geometrization Conjecture (Thurston), classifying all closed 3-manifolds.

Resolution: Perelman's work was verified by Kleiner–Lott, Cao–Zhu, Morgan–Tian, and others. The Millennium Prize was offered; Perelman declined.

Related: Topology, Differential Geometry, Smooth Manifolds.

6) Riemann Hypothesis, Yang–Mills, Navier–Stokes, BSD

Riemann Hypothesis

All non-trivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\) lie on \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}\). Implies optimal prime distribution error terms. Research guide.

Yang–Mills mass gap

Construct rigorous 4D quantum Yang–Mills theory with positive mass gap \(\Delta > 0\). Foundation of QCD confinement. Research guide.

Navier–Stokes

Prove existence and smoothness of solutions to the 3D incompressible Navier–Stokes equations for all smooth initial data, or exhibit a counterexample. Related: Partial Differential Equations.

Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer

\(\operatorname{ord}_{s=1} L(E, s) = \mathrm{rank}(E)\) for elliptic curves \(E/\mathbb{Q}\). Weak BSD known for rank 0 and 1. Research guide.
