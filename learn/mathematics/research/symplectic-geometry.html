1) Symplectic forms and symplectic manifolds

A symplectic form on a smooth \(2n\)-dimensional manifold \(M\) is a closed nondegenerate 2-form \(\omega \in \Omega^2(M)\): \[ d\omega = 0, \qquad \omega^n \neq 0 \text{ pointwise.} \] The pair \((M, \omega)\) is a symplectic manifold. Locally, Darboux's theorem guarantees coordinates \((q_1,\ldots,q_n,p_1,\ldots,p_n)\) with \[ \omega = \sum_{i=1}^n dq_i \wedge dp_i. \]

Nondegeneracy induces an isomorphism \(\flat : TM \to T^*M\), \(X \mapsto \iota_X \omega\). A vector field \(X\) is symplectic if \(\mathcal{L}_X \omega = 0\); it is Hamiltonian if \(X = X_H\) for some \(H \in C^\infty(M)\) with \(\iota_{X_H}\omega = dH\).

Key example: Phase space \((T^*Q, \omega_{\mathrm{can}})\) with \(\omega_{\mathrm{can}} = \sum dq_i \wedge dp_i\) is the archetype. Cotangent bundles carry canonical symplectic structures for any configuration manifold \(Q\).

2) Hamiltonian mechanics and Poisson brackets

For \(H : M \to \mathbb{R}\), Hamilton's equations in Darboux coordinates are \[ \dot{q}_i = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_i}, \qquad \dot{p}_i = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q_i}. \] Equivalently, flow along \(X_H\) satisfies \(\iota_{X_H}\omega = dH\). The Poisson bracket of functions \(f, g\) is \[ \{f, g\} = \omega(X_f, X_g) = \sum_i \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial q_i}\frac{\partial g}{\partial p_i} - \frac{\partial f}{\partial p_i}\frac{\partial g}{\partial q_i}\right). \]

Liouville's theorem: Hamiltonian flow preserves \(\omega^n\). Phase-space volume is invariant under time evolution — the geometric foundation of statistical mechanics on symplectic manifolds.
Conservation laws: If \(\{H, f\} = 0\), then \(f\) is conserved along the flow of \(H\). Noether's theorem in this setting: symmetries of \(H\) yield conserved quantities via moment maps.

Integrability

A system with \(n\) independent commuting Hamiltonians \(H_1, \ldots, H_n\) (including \(H\)) is Liouville integrable; level sets are Lagrangian tori with linear flow.

Arnold–Liouville

Near a compact regular level set of an integrable system, action-angle coordinates exist: the geometry is explicitly toroidal.

Convention: Sign conventions for \(\omega\), Poisson brackets, and complex structures vary across texts. Always verify whether \(\{q_i, p_j\} = \delta_{ij}\) or \(-\delta_{ij}\).

3) Lagrangian submanifolds and moment maps

A submanifold \(L \subset M\) is Lagrangian if \(\dim L = n\) and \(\omega|_L = 0\). In \(T^*Q\), graphs of closed 1-forms and zero sections are Lagrangian. Lagrangian submanifolds are central to Homological Mirror Symmetry via the Fukaya category.

For a Lie group \(G\) acting on \((M, \omega)\) by symplectomorphisms, a moment map \(\mu : M \to \mathfrak{g}^*\) satisfies \[ \langle \mu(x), \xi \rangle = \text{Hamiltonian for } X_\xi, \qquad \iota_{X_\xi}\omega = d\langle \mu, \xi \rangle. \] Marsden–Weinstein reduction produces a symplectic quotient \(M /\!\!/ G\) at regular values of \(\mu\).

Worked example: For \(G = \mathrm{U}(1)\) acting on \(\mathbb{C}^n\) by diagonal phase rotations with standard \(\omega\), \(\mu(z) = \frac{1}{2}\sum |z_i|^2\). The reduced space at \(\mu = 1\) is \(\mathbb{CP}^{n-1}\) with Fubini–Study form.

Symplectic capacity

Gromov's non-squeezing constrains how symplectic balls embed into cylinders — symplectic rigidity absent in volume-preserving topology.

Contact geometry

A contact manifold \((Y, \xi)\) is a \(2n{+}1\)-dimensional boundary theory; \(\xi = \ker \alpha\) with \(d\alpha\) symplectic on \(\xi\).

4) Almost complex structures and J-holomorphic curves

An almost complex structure \(J\) on \((M, \omega)\) is \(\omega\)-compatible if \(g(v,w) = \omega(v, Jw)\) is positive definite. Gromov's innovation (1985) studies \(J\)-holomorphic curves: maps \(u : \Sigma \to M\) from a Riemann surface satisfying \[ \bar{\partial}_J u = \frac{1}{2}(\partial u + J \circ du \circ j) = 0. \] These are minimal surfaces in a non-integrable almost complex setting and replace geodesics as the preferred 1-dimensional objects.

Moduli problem: For fixed genus \(g\), homology class \(A \in H_2(M)\), and compatible \(J\), the space \(\mathcal{M}(g,A,J)\) of \(J\)-holomorphic curves is a (stratified) manifold of dimension \[ 6g - 6 + 2n + 2\langle c_1(TM), A \rangle. \] Compactification via stable maps (Deligne–Mumford) yields Gromov–Witten theory.
Transversality: For generic \(J\), the moduli space is smooth; automatic transversality holds in dimension 4 under certain conditions. Bubbling and Fredholm index computations are the technical core.

5) Gromov–Witten invariants: preview

Gromov–Witten invariants count (appropriately weighted) \(J\)-holomorphic curves in \(M\) representing a class \(A \in H_2(M;\mathbb{Z})\) with prescribed marked points. For genus zero: \[ \mathrm{GW}_{0,k}(M, A; \alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_k) = \int_{[\mathcal{M}_{0,k}(M,A)]^{\mathrm{vir}}} \mathrm{ev}_1^*\alpha_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \mathrm{ev}_k^*\alpha_k. \] The virtual fundamental class compensates for the failure of \(\mathcal{M}\) to be compact or smooth.

Quantum cohomology: GW invariants deform the cup product on \(H^*(M)\) into a quantum product \(\star\), encoding rational curves. For \(\mathbb{CP}^n\), quantum cohomology recovers enumerative data (Kontsevich's rational curves formula).

6) Floer theory and holomorphic discs

Floer homology replaces Morse homology with infinite-dimensional gradient flows on loop spaces or with holomorphic strips between Lagrangians. For a pair \((L_0, L_1)\) of Lagrangian submanifolds, the Fukaya category \(\mathcal{F}(M)\) has objects = Lagrangians, morphisms = Floer complexes generated by intersection points.

Mirror symmetry bridge: Kontsevich's HMS conjecture identifies \(\mathcal{F}(M)\) with \(\mathbf{D}^b\mathrm{Coh}(W)\) for a mirror partner \(W\). Counting holomorphic discs (open GW invariants) matches period integrals on \(W\).

Symplectic field theory

SFT packages holomorphic curves in symplectic cobordisms into a \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) algebra over a contact homology algebra — a grand unified framework for curve counting.

Hofer's energy

For a \(J\)-holomorphic curve \(u\), \(E(u) = \int u^*\omega \geq 0\). Finite energy and properness control compactness via Gromov's compactness theorem.

7) Research connections and frontier directions

Mirror symmetry

Symplectic \(A\)-model (GW, Fukaya) dual to complex \(B\)-model (periods, variation of Hodge structure). See Homological Mirror Symmetry.

Topological recursion

GW potentials of toric Calabi–Yau threefolds satisfy Eynard–Orantin topological recursion, linking symplectic enumerative geometry to random matrices.

Symplectic 4-manifolds

Gromov non-squeezing, Taubes's SW=GW, and existence of symplectic structures on 4-manifolds remain active classification problems.

Hamiltonian dynamics

KAM theory, Aubry–Mather sets, and symplectic capacities (Ekeland–Hofer) connect rigidity to celestial mechanics and PDE.

Related genius-level topics: Homological Mirror Symmetry, Cohomology, Random Matrices, Differential Geometry.
