P-adic Analysis

Postgraduate guide. P-adic numbers, ultrametric analysis, Hensel's lemma, p-adic power series, and local methods in number theory.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Construction of \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) and \(\mathbb{Z}_p\)

For a prime \(p\), the p-adic valuation \(v_p: \mathbb{Q} \to \mathbb{Z} \cup \{\infty\}\) counts the power of \(p\) in a rational. The p-adic absolute value is: \[ |x|_p = p^{-v_p(x)}, \quad |0|_p = 0 \] The field \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) is the completion of \(\mathbb{Q}\) with respect to \(|\cdot|_p\).

Key idea: In \(\mathbb{Q}_p\), a series \(\sum a_n\) converges if and only if \(a_n \to 0\) — much simpler than the Archimedean case. Large powers of \(p\) are "small."
Integers: \(\mathbb{Z}_p = \{x \in \mathbb{Q}_p : |x|_p \leq 1\}\) are exactly \(p\)-adic integers; every \(x \in \mathbb{Z}_p\) has a unique expansion \(x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n p^n\) with \(a_n \in \{0, \ldots, p-1\}\).

Prerequisite: Convergence and Algebraic Number Theory.

2) Ultrametric spaces and topology

The p-adic absolute value satisfies the ultrametric inequality: \[ |x + y|_p \leq \max(|x|_p, |y|_p) \] Every triangle is isosceles in \(\mathbb{Q}_p\): if \(|x|_p \neq |y|_p\), then \(|x+y|_p = \max(|x|_p, |y|_p)\).

Topology: \(\mathbb{Z}_p\) and \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) are totally disconnected locally compact Hausdorff spaces. Open balls in \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) are also closed.
Common mistake: Applying Archimedean intuition — "small" means high \(p\)-divisibility, not proximity to zero in the usual real sense.

3) Hensel's lemma

Hensel's lemma is the p-adic analogue of the implicit function theorem for polynomials. If \(f \in \mathbb{Z}_p[x]\), \(f(a) \equiv 0 \pmod{p}\) with \(f'(a) \not\equiv 0 \pmod{p}\), then there exists a unique \(b \in \mathbb{Z}_p\) with \(f(b) = 0\) and \(b \equiv a \pmod{p}\).

Newton lifting: Iterate \(a_{n+1} = a_n - f(a_n)/f'(a_n)\) in \(\mathbb{Q}_p\); each step improves \(p\)-adic accuracy by one digit.
Worked example: Solve \(x^2 = 2\) in \(\mathbb{Z}_7\). Start with \(a_0 = 3\) since \(3^2 = 9 \equiv 2 \pmod{7}\). Hensel lifting gives a unique 7-adic square root of 2.

4) P-adic power series and analytic functions

A formal power series \(f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n x^n\) converges on the largest open ball \(\{x : |x|_p < R\}\) where \(R = 1/\limsup |c_n|_p^{1/n}\). Entire p-adic functions converge on all of \(\mathbb{Q}_p\).

Exponential and logarithm: Define \[ \exp_p(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!}, \quad \log_p(1+x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1} x^n}{n} \] convergent for \(|x|_p < p^{-1/(p-1)}\) (and adjusted in characteristic \(p\) settings).
Mahler expansion: Continuous functions \(f: \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{Q}_p\) admit expansions in binomial coefficient bases — a discrete analogue of Taylor series.

5) Local-global principles

A central theme: understand global objects over \(\mathbb{Q}\) by studying all completions \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) and \(\mathbb{R}\). Hasse's principle holds for some classes (quadratic forms) but fails in general.

Adeles: The ring of adeles \(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q} = \mathbb{R} \times \prod_p' \mathbb{Q}_p\) packages all local fields; automorphic forms and L-functions are naturally adelic.
Counterexample: The curve \(3x^3 + 4y^3 + 5z^3 = 0\) has nontrivial solutions over every \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) and \(\mathbb{R}\), but no nontrivial rational solution — Hasse principle failure.

Related: Automorphic Forms and Algebraic Number Theory.

6) P-adic integration and measure

On \(\mathbb{Z}_p\), the Haar measure \(\mu_p\) is normalised with \(\mu_p(\mathbb{Z}_p) = 1\). Balls have measure \(p^{-n}\) where \(n\) is the radius exponent. This enables p-adic integration theory.

Volkenborn integral

Integrates uniformly continuous functions on \(\mathbb{Z}_p\); linked to Bernoulli numbers and zeta values.

Igusa local zeta

Counts p-adic solution fibres to polynomial equations; rational in \(p^{-s}\) with poles encoding singularities.

7) Applications

Diophantine geometry

p-adic methods constrain rational points on curves; Chabauty–Coleman techniques use p-adic integrals.

Cryptography

Some protocols use p-adic towers and Lubin–Tate formal groups; padic arithmetic appears in error detection.

Physics

p-adic strings and adelic quantum mechanics explore non-Archimedean spacetime models.

Representation theory

Local Langlands correspondence is formulated over \(\mathbb{Q}_p\) before global assembly.
