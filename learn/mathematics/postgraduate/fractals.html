1) What is a fractal?

A fractal is typically a set exhibiting self-similarity at multiple scales, fine structure at arbitrarily small scales, and often non-integer dimension. Mandelbrot's definition emphasises sets whose Hausdorff dimension strictly exceeds the topological dimension.

Key idea: Fractals model phenomena where Euclidean geometry fails — coastlines, turbulence, porous media, and aggregate growth. "Dimension" becomes a continuum parameter, not just 0, 1, 2, or 3.

Prerequisite: Euclidean Space and Measure Theory (for rigorous dimension theory).

2) Fractal dimensions

Dimension Definition sketch Example
Topological Inductive definition from neighbourhoods Cantor set: 0
Hausdorff Measure-theoretic scaling exponent Koch curve: \(\log 4 / \log 3\)
Box-counting \(N(\varepsilon) \sim \varepsilon^{-D}\) as \(\varepsilon \to 0\) Sierpiński gasket: \(\log 3 / \log 2\)
Correlation Scaling of mass distribution Used in time-series analysis
Worked example — Cantor set: Remove middle thirds iteratively from \([0,1]\). At stage \(n\), \(2^n\) intervals of length \(3^{-n}\) remain. Box-counting gives \(D = \dfrac{\log 2}{\log 3} \approx 0.631\). Hausdorff dimension agrees.
Convention: Box-counting and Hausdorff dimensions need not coincide for general sets. Self-similar sets satisfying open separation condition have equal Hausdorff and similarity dimension.

3) Iterated function systems (IFS)

An iterated function system on \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is a finite collection of contractions \(\{w_i\}_{i=1}^N\). The Hutchinson operator \(H(A) = \bigcup_i w_i(A)\) has a unique attractor \(F\) — the fractal — by Banach's fixed-point theorem on the space of compact sets with Hausdorff metric.

Worked example — Sierpiński triangle: Three contractions scaling by \(\tfrac{1}{2}\) toward vertices of a triangle. The attractor is the Sierpiński gasket with similarity dimension \(D = \log 3 / \log 2\).
Collage theorem: Given a target image, one can approximate it as an IFS attractor — basis of fractal image compression.

4) Classical fractal constructions

Koch snowflake: Replace each segment with four segments of length \(1/3\). Perimeter \(\to \infty\) but enclosed area is finite. Hausdorff dimension \(\log 4 / \log 3 \approx 1.262\).
Menger sponge: 3D analogue of Cantor set — dimension \(\log 20 / \log 3 \approx 2.727\).
Space-filling curves: Peano and Hilbert curves are continuous surjections \([0,1] \to [0,1]^2\). They are fractal in the sense of non-differentiability and scaling self-similarity (Hilbert).

Related: Homeomorphism — topological dimension vs Hausdorff dimension.

5) Julia sets and the Mandelbrot set

For \(f_c(z) = z^2 + c\), the filled Julia set \(K_c\) is the set of bounded orbits; the Julia set \(J_c = \partial K_c\) is the boundary. The Mandelbrot set \(\mathcal{M} = \{c \in \mathbb{C} : J_c \text{ is connected}\}\).

Worked example: For \(c = 0\), \(J_0 = S^1\) (unit circle). For \(c = -2\), dynamics collapse to an interval. For \(c = i\), \(J_c\) is a dendrite — fractal boundary with Hausdorff dimension \(> 1\).
Connection to chaos: On \(J_c\), the dynamics of \(f_c\) is chaotic (sensitive dependence, dense periodic points). Julia sets link Complex Functions to Chaos Theory.

6) Applications in science and engineering

Porous media

Permeability and diffusion on fractal pore networks; anomalous diffusion with exponent tied to fractal dimension.

Signal processing

1/f noise, multifractal analysis of turbulence and financial time series; wavelet leaders estimate local scaling exponents.

Antennas and metamaterials

Fractal antenna designs achieve wideband or multiband behaviour in compact form factors.

Computer graphics

Procedural terrain (fractional Brownian motion), L-systems for botanical structures, and IFS rendering.

Related: Statistics, Nonlinear Dynamics.

7) Measure theory and multifractals

Hausdorff measure \(\mathcal{H}^s\) generalises length, area, and volume to non-integer \(s\). The Hausdorff dimension is the critical exponent where \(\mathcal{H}^s\) jumps from \(\infty\) to 0.

Multifractal formalism: A measure \(\mu\) on a fractal may have varying local scaling \(\mu(B_r(x)) \sim r^{\alpha(x)}\). The spectrum \(f(\alpha)\) of Hölder exponents describes heterogeneity beyond a single dimension number.
Research context: Percolation clusters, Brownian motion path (dimension 2 a.s. in the plane), and Schramm–Loewner evolution (SLE) connect fractals to probability and conformal invariance.
