Smooth Manifolds

Postgraduate guide. Charts and atlases, smooth structures, tangent bundles, vector fields, and differential forms on manifolds.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Topological manifolds and charts

An \(n\)-dimensional topological manifold \(M\) is a Hausdorff, second-countable space locally homeomorphic to \(\mathbb{R}^n\). A chart \((U, \varphi)\) is an open set \(U \subset M\) with homeomorphism \(\varphi: U \to \varphi(U) \subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Atlas: A collection \(\{(U_\alpha, \varphi_\alpha)\}\) covering \(M\) whose transition maps \(\varphi_\beta \circ \varphi_\alpha^{-1}\) are smooth where defined. A smooth structure is a maximal smooth atlas.
Examples: \(\mathbb{R}^n\), the sphere \(S^n\), tori \(T^n\), and Lie groups such as \(\mathrm{GL}(n,\mathbb{R})\) and \(\mathrm{SO}(n)\) are smooth manifolds.

Prerequisite: Topology and Differentiation.

2) Smooth maps and diffeomorphisms

A map \(F: M \to N\) between smooth manifolds is smooth if for every chart \((U, \varphi)\) on \(M\) and \((V, \psi)\) on \(N\), the composition \(\psi \circ F \circ \varphi^{-1}\) is smooth as a map between open subsets of Euclidean space.

Diffeomorphism: A smooth bijection with smooth inverse. Diffeomorphic manifolds are regarded as smoothly equivalent — the natural notion of isomorphism in differential topology.
Common mistake: Confusing topological homeomorphism with diffeomorphism. Exotic spheres provide homeomorphic but non-diffeomorphic smooth structures in dimension \(\geq 4\).

3) Tangent spaces and the tangent bundle

At \(p \in M\), the tangent space \(T_pM\) is the vector space of derivations at \(p\): \(\mathbb{R}\)-linear maps \(v: C^\infty(M) \to \mathbb{R}\) satisfying the Leibniz rule. In coordinates \((x^i)\), \(\{\partial/\partial x^i|_p\}\) is a basis.

Tangent bundle: \(TM = \bigsqcup_{p \in M} T_pM\) is a \(2n\)-manifold with projection \(\pi: TM \to M\). A vector field is a smooth section \(X: M \to TM\).
Differential of a map: For smooth \(F: M \to N\), the pushforward \(dF_p: T_pM \to T_{F(p)}N\) is defined by \(dF_p(v)(f) = v(f \circ F)\).

4) Vector fields and Lie bracket

The space \(\mathfrak{X}(M)\) of smooth vector fields is a Lie algebra under the Lie bracket: \[ [X, Y]f = X(Yf) - Y(Xf) \] Flows of vector fields generate one-parameter families of diffeomorphisms.

Integral curves: A curve \(\gamma\) with \(\dot{\gamma}(t) = X(\gamma(t))\) is an integral curve of \(X\). Existence and uniqueness follow from ODE theory in charts.
Worked example: On \(\mathbb{R}^2\), \(X = x\partial/\partial x + y\partial/\partial y\) generates scaling about the origin: its flow is \(\phi_t(x,y) = (e^t x, e^t y)\).

Related: Differential Equations and Lie Algebras.

5) Differential forms and exterior calculus

A \(k\)-form on \(M\) is a smooth section of \(\bigwedge^k T^*M\). The exterior derivative \(d\) satisfies \(d^2 = 0\) and generalises gradient, curl, and divergence in \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

de Rham cohomology: \(H^k_{\mathrm{dR}}(M) = \ker d / \mathrm{im}\, d\) measures global topological obstructions to exactness. For compact \(M\), \(H^k_{\mathrm{dR}}(M) \cong H^k(M; \mathbb{R})\).
Stokes' theorem on manifolds: \[ \int_M d\omega = \int_{\partial M} \omega \] Unifies Stokes' Theorem and the divergence theorem as one statement.

6) Submanifolds and transversality

A subset \(S \subset M\) is a embedded submanifold if it inherits a smooth structure making the inclusion \(S \hookrightarrow M\) a smooth embedding. The Regular Value Theorem states: if \(F: M \to N\) is smooth and \(q \in N\) is a regular value of \(F\), then \(F^{-1}(q)\) is a submanifold of dimension \(\dim M - \dim N\).

Transversality: Submanifolds \(X, Y \subset M\) intersect transversally if \(T_p X + T_p Y = T_p M\) at each intersection point \(p\). Transversality is stable under small perturbations.

Immersions and embeddings

Immersions have injective differential; embeddings are injective immersions with the subspace topology.

Whitney embedding

Every smooth \(n\)-manifold embeds in \(\mathbb{R}^{2n}\).

7) Applications

General relativity

Spacetime is a 4-manifold with Lorentzian metric; geodesics and curvature tensors live on tangent and tensor bundles.

Robotics and mechanics

Configuration spaces of joints are manifolds; Lie groups model rigid-body motions.

Differential geometry

Connections, curvature, and characteristic classes extend calculus to curved spaces.

Topology

Morse theory, index theory, and surgery relate smooth structure to global invariants.

Related: Differential Geometry, Vector Calculus.
