1) Modular forms as the prototype

A modular form of weight \(k\) on \(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})\) is a holomorphic function \(f\) on the upper half-plane \(\mathbb{H}\) satisfying: \[ f(\gamma z) = (cz + d)^k f(z), \quad \gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) \] and a growth condition at the cusp.

Fourier expansion: At the cusp \(\infty\), \[ f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n e^{2\pi i n z} \] A cusp form has \(a_0 = 0\). The coefficients \(a_n\) encode deep arithmetic information.
Worked example: The Eisenstein series \(E_k(z)\) of weight \(k \geq 4\) even is a modular form with \(a_0 \neq 0\); the discriminant \(\Delta(z) = q\prod(1-q^n)^{24}\) is a weight-12 cusp form.

Prerequisite: Complex Analysis and Group Theory.

2) Automorphic forms on higher groups

An automorphic form on a reductive algebraic group \(G\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\) is a function on \(G(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q})\) (the adele group) transforming under a character of a Borel subgroup, satisfying moderate growth and invariance under a discrete subgroup \(\Gamma \subset G(\mathbb{Q})\).

Key idea: Automorphic forms unify modular forms, Maass forms, and theta series as instances of representation-theoretic functions on \(G(\mathbb{A})\).
Level and character: Congruence subgroups \(\Gamma_0(N)\), \(\Gamma_1(N)\) generalise \(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})\); nebentypus characters \(\psi\) modify the transformation law by a root of unity.

3) Hecke operators and multiplicativity

Hecke operators \(T_p\) act on spaces of modular forms, preserving cusp forms. On Fourier coefficients: \[ a_n(T_p f) = a_{np}(f) + p^{k-1} a_{n/p}(f) \] for prime \(p\) and weight \(k\).

Eigenforms: A normalized Hecke eigenform satisfies \(T_p f = a_p f\) for all primes \(p\). Then \(a_{mn} = a_m a_n\) when \((m,n)=1\), and \(a_{p^{r+1}} = a_p a_{p^r} - p^{k-1} a_{p^{r-1}}\).
Worked example: The Ramanujan \(\tau(n)\) coefficients of \(\Delta(z)\) satisfy \(\tau(mn) = \tau(m)\tau(n)\) for \((m,n)=1\) and \(\tau(p^{r+1}) = \tau(p)\tau(p^r) - p^{11}\tau(p^{r-1})\).

4) L-functions attached to automorphic forms

For a cusp form \(f\) with coefficients \(a_n\), the L-function is: \[ L(f, s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{a_n}{n^s} = \prod_p \frac{1}{1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{k-1-2s}} \] convergent for \(\mathrm{Re}(s)\) large, with meromorphic continuation and functional equation.

Functional equation: Completed L-function \(\Lambda(f,s)\) satisfies \(\Lambda(f, s) = \varepsilon \Lambda(\tilde{f}, 1-s)\) with root number \(\varepsilon\) and dual form \(\tilde{f}\).
Common mistake: Confusing the Dirichlet series \(\sum a_n n^{-s}\) with the Dedekind zeta of a number field — automorphic L-functions arise from analytic objects on groups, not only ideals.

5) Langlands correspondence (overview)

The Langlands programme predicts a correspondence between:

  • Galois representations \(\rho: \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{C})\)
  • Automorphic representations \(\pi\) of \(\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q})\)

with compatible L-functions: \(L(\rho, s) = L(\pi, s)\).

Modularity theorem (Wiles et al.): Every semistable elliptic curve over \(\mathbb{Q}\) is modular — its L-function equals \(L(f,s)\) for a weight-2 cusp form \(f\). This was essential to Fermat's Last Theorem.

Related: Algebraic Number Theory and Riemann Hypothesis.

6) Spectral theory and automorphic representations

Automorphic forms arise as matrix coefficients of unitary automorphic representations in \(L^2(G(\mathbb{Q})\backslash G(\mathbb{A}))\). The spectral decomposition involves:

Cusp forms

Square-integrable, rapid decay at cusps; discrete spectrum contributions.

Eisenstein series

Continuous spectrum built from parabolic induction and Rankin–Selberg integrals.

Rankin–Selberg method: Integrals of products of Eisenstein series against cusp forms compute L-function special values and analytic continuation.

7) Applications

Number theory

Distribution of primes, reciprocity laws, and Birch–Swinnerton–Dyer conjecture use automorphic L-functions.

Physics

Partition functions, black-hole entropy (BPS states), and moonshine connect modular forms to conformal field theory.

Cryptography

L-functions of curves underpin security assumptions; modular forms appear in isogeny-based protocols.

Harmonic analysis

Theta functions and Poisson summation are automorphic forms on metaplectic groups.
