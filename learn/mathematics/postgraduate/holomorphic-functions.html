Holomorphic Functions

Postgraduate guide. Equivalent characterizations of holomorphy, power series, identity and maximum principles, Schwarz lemma, and automorphisms of the disc.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Equivalent definitions of holomorphy

For \(f : D \to \mathbb{C}\) on an open set \(D\), the following are equivalent (when \(D\) is connected, (1)–(5) are locally equivalent; global equivalences need connectedness):

  1. \(f\) is complex differentiable at every point of \(D\).
  2. \(f\) is holomorphic (analytic) on \(D\) in the sense of complex differentiability.
  3. \(f\) is real-differentiable and satisfies the Cauchy–Riemann equations with continuous partial derivatives.
  4. \(f\) is locally equal to a convergent power series on \(D\).
  5. \(f\) is infinitely complex differentiable on \(D\).
Key idea: In one complex variable, differentiability once implies \(C^\infty\) and local analyticity — a dramatic contrast with real analysis, where \(C^\infty\) does not imply real-analyticity.

Prerequisite: Complex Analysis and Real Analysis.

2) Power series and analyticity

If \(f\) is holomorphic on \(|z - z_0| < R\), then \(f\) has a Taylor series \[ f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (z - z_0)^n, \quad a_n = \frac{f^{(n)}(z_0)}{n!} \] converging to \(f\) on the largest open disc centred at \(z_0\) contained in \(D\) (radius of convergence until the first singularity).

Worked example: \(f(z) = \dfrac{1}{1-z}\) has Taylor series \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} z^n\) for \(|z| < 1\). The pole at \(z = 1\) determines the radius of convergence.
Morera's theorem: If \(\oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 0\) for every closed contour \(\gamma\) in a domain \(D\), then \(f\) is holomorphic on \(D\). Converse of Cauchy's theorem in simply connected domains.
Common mistake: A power series converging on a disc defines a holomorphic function inside, but the function may extend holomorphically beyond the disc (analytic continuation). The series boundary is not necessarily a natural boundary.

3) The identity theorem and analytic continuation

The identity theorem: if \(f\) and \(g\) are holomorphic on a connected open set \(D\) and agree on a set with a limit point in \(D\), then \(f \equiv g\) on \(D\).

Consequence: A holomorphic function on a connected domain is determined by its values on any small open subset, or on any sequence converging to a point in the domain.
Analytic continuation: If \(f_1\) is holomorphic on \(D_1\) and \(f_2\) on \(D_2\) with \(D_1 \cap D_2\) connected and \(f_1 = f_2\) there, the glued function is holomorphic on \(D_1 \cup D_2\). Maximal domains are domains of holomorphy (in one variable, every domain is a domain of holomorphy).

4) Maximum modulus principle

If \(f\) is holomorphic and non-constant on a bounded open set \(D\), then \(|f|\) attains its maximum only on \(\partial D\). Equivalently, \(|f(z)| < \sup_{\partial D}|f|\) for all \(z \in D\).

Proof sketch: If \(|f|\) has an interior maximum at \(z_0\), then \(g(z) = f(z)/f(z_0)\) is holomorphic with \(|g| \leq 1\) and \(g(z_0) = 1\). By the maximum principle for harmonic functions applied to \(\log|g|\) (where \(g \neq 0\)), \(g\) is constant.
Phragmén–Lindelöf principle: Extends maximum principle to unbounded domains (e.g. strips), crucial for bounding entire functions of exponential type.
Convention: The minimum modulus principle is false for holomorphic functions (zeros occur). Apply to \(1/f\) only where \(f \neq 0\).

5) Schwarz lemma and automorphisms of the disc

Let \(\mathbb{D} = \{z : |z| < 1\}\). If \(f : \mathbb{D} \to \mathbb{D}\) is holomorphic with \(f(0) = 0\), then \(|f(z)| \leq |z|\) and \(|f'(0)| \leq 1\). Equality at some \(z \neq 0\) iff \(f\) is a rotation \(f(z) = e^{i\theta} z\).

Automorphisms of \(\mathbb{D}\): Every conformal self-map of \(\mathbb{D}\) has the form \[ \varphi_a(z) = \frac{z - a}{1 - \bar{a}z}, \quad |a| < 1 \] (possibly composed with a rotation). The group \(\operatorname{Aut}(\mathbb{D}) \cong PSU(1,1)\).
Riemann mapping theorem (recall): Any simply connected proper subdomain of \(\mathbb{C}\) is conformally equivalent to \(\mathbb{D}\). Holomorphic bijections are rare and highly constrained.

Schwarz–Pick lemma

For holomorphic \(f : \mathbb{D} \to \mathbb{D}\), the hyperbolic metric is non-increasing: distances decrease under \(f\). Generalizes Schwarz lemma.

Fixed points

A holomorphic self-map of \(\mathbb{D}\) (not identity) has at most one fixed point in \(\mathbb{D}\) (Denjoy–Wolff theorem describes iteration limits).

6) Normal families, Runge, and Mittag-Leffler

A family \(\mathcal{F}\) of holomorphic functions on \(D\) is normal if every sequence has a subsequence converging uniformly on compact subsets (to a holomorphic limit or \(\infty\)).

Montel's theorem: A locally uniformly bounded family of holomorphic functions on \(D\) is normal. Contrast with Arzelà–Ascoli in real analysis (equicontinuity also needed).
Runge's theorem: On a compact \(K \subset \mathbb{C}\) with connected complement, every function holomorphic on a neighbourhood of \(K\) is a uniform limit of rational functions with poles off \(K\).
Mittag-Leffler's theorem: Given poles \(\{a_n\}\) and principal parts at each pole, there exists a meromorphic function with exactly those singularities. Dual to Weierstrass product theorem for zeros.

These approximation and construction theorems are pillars of function theory and underpin the theory of several complex variables.

7) Applications and research outlook

Complex dynamics

Iteration \(f^n(z)\) of holomorphic maps on \(\mathbb{C}\) or \(\hat{\mathbb{C}}\) produces Julia and Mandelbrot sets — fractal boundaries between basins of attraction.

Hardy spaces

\(H^p(\mathbb{D})\): holomorphic functions with bounded \(L^p\) boundary values. Factorization \(f = BS \cdot g\) (Blaschke product × singular inner × outer).

Several variables

Hartogs extension: holomorphic functions extend across compact obstacles. No global Riemann mapping theorem for \(n \geq 2\).

Physics

Holomorphic structures on Kähler manifolds in string theory. Two-dimensional conformal field theory uses meromorphic correlation functions.

Related: Complex Analysis, Riemann Surfaces, Differential Geometry.
