1) Normed vector spaces

A normed vector space \((X, \|\cdot\|)\) over \(\mathbb{R}\) or \(\mathbb{C}\) is a vector space equipped with a norm satisfying positivity, homogeneity, and the triangle inequality: \[ \|x\| \geq 0,\quad \|\lambda x\| = |\lambda|\|x\|,\quad \|x+y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\| \]

Examples:
  • \(\mathbb{R}^n\) with \(\|x\|_p = \left(\sum |x_i|^p\right)^{1/p}\), \(1 \leq p \leq \infty\)
  • \(C([a,b])\) with \(\|f\|_\infty = \sup_{t \in [a,b]} |f(t)|\)
  • \(L^p(X,\mu)\) from Measure Theory
Induced metric: \(d(x,y) = \|x - y\|\) makes \(X\) a metric space. Convergence and Cauchy sequences are defined via this metric.

Prerequisite: Vector Spaces, Convergence.

2) Banach spaces: completeness

A Banach space is a normed vector space that is complete: every Cauchy sequence converges in \(X\).

Key examples: \(\mathbb{R}^n\), \(L^p(\mu)\) for \(1 \leq p \leq \infty\), and \(C([a,b])\) with \(\|\cdot\|_\infty\) are Banach spaces. The rationals with absolute value norm are not complete.
Completion: Every normed space embeds densely in a Banach space (its completion), constructed via equivalence classes of Cauchy sequences — analogous to constructing \(\mathbb{R}\) from \(\mathbb{Q}\).
Distinction: All norms on finite-dimensional spaces are equivalent, but infinite-dimensional spaces admit non-equivalent norms with different completeness properties.

3) Bounded linear operators

A linear map \(T: X \to Y\) between normed spaces is bounded if \[ \|T\| = \sup_{\|x\| \leq 1} \|Tx\| < \infty \] Boundedness is equivalent to continuity at 0 (hence everywhere).

Operator space \(\mathcal{B}(X,Y)\): Bounded operators form a Banach space with operator norm. When \(Y = \mathbb{C}\) (or \(\mathbb{R}\)), the dual space \(X^* = \mathcal{B}(X, \mathbb{C})\) is Banach.
Worked example: Integration \(T(f) = \int_a^b f(t)\,dt\) is a bounded linear functional on \((C([a,b]), \|\cdot\|_\infty)\) with \(\|T\| = b - a\).

4) Hahn–Banach theorem

The Hahn–Banach theorem is the fundamental extension result of functional analysis.

Analytic form: A bounded linear functional on a subspace of a Banach space extends to the whole space without increasing the norm.
Geometric form: A convex open set and a point not in its closure can be separated by a continuous linear functional — the basis for duality and optimality conditions.
Consequence: \(X\) embeds isometrically in \(X^{**}\) (the bidual). Reflexive spaces satisfy \(X \cong X^{**}\) — e.g. \(L^p\) for \(1 < p < \infty\).

5) Open mapping and closed graph theorems

For Banach spaces, algebraic properties of operators intertwine with topology.

Open mapping theorem: A surjective bounded linear operator between Banach spaces is open (maps open sets to open sets). In particular, a bijective bounded operator has a bounded inverse.
Closed graph theorem: A linear operator \(T: X \to Y\) with closed graph is bounded, provided \(X\) and \(Y\) are Banach. This simplifies proving boundedness for operators defined by limits or integrals.
Uniform boundedness (Banach–Steinhaus): A pointwise bounded family of operators from a Banach space is uniformly bounded in operator norm.

6) Dual spaces and weak topologies

The dual space \(X^*\) consists of continuous linear functionals. The weak topology \(\sigma(X, X^*)\) is the coarsest topology making all functionals in \(X^*\) continuous.

Weak* topology: On \(X^*\), the topology \(\sigma(X^*, X)\) is the weak-star topology. Alaoglu's theorem: the closed unit ball of \(X^*\) is weak*-compact (for Banach \(X\)).
Reflexivity and compactness: Weak compactness of bounded sets characterises reflexive Banach spaces and underpins existence proofs in PDE and Control Theory.

7) Applications in analysis and engineering

PDE and variational methods

Sobolev spaces \(W^{k,p}\) are Banach spaces. Existence of weak solutions to elliptic PDEs uses coercivity and Lax–Milgram in Hilbert settings.

Signal and control

\(L^p\) and \(H^\infty\) spaces model signals and transfer operators. Robust control minimises norms in Banach operator spaces.

Related: Hilbert Spaces, Measure Theory, Control Theory.
