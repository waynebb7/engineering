1) Infinitesimal transformations and vector fields

A one-parameter family of transformations \(T_\varepsilon : M \to M\) with \(T_0 = \mathrm{id}\) has an infinitesimal generator — a vector field \(\mathbf{X}\) on \(M\). In coordinates \((x^1, \ldots, x^n)\): \[ X = \xi^i(x) \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i} \] The first-order effect of \(T_\varepsilon\) on a function \(f\) is \(\varepsilon \mathbf{X}(f) + O(\varepsilon^2)\).

Key idea: Finite symmetries are obtained by exponentiating infinitesimal generators: \(T_\varepsilon = \exp(\varepsilon \mathbf{X})\) (flow of \(\mathbf{X}\)).

Prerequisite: Vector Calculus, Differential Equations.

2) Flow and the exponential map

The flow \(\phi_t^\mathbf{X}\) of a vector field \(\mathbf{X}\) satisfies \(\dfrac{d}{dt}\phi_t^\mathbf{X}(x) = \mathbf{X}(\phi_t^\mathbf{X}(x))\) and \(\phi_0^\mathbf{X}(x) = x\). For linear \(\mathbf{X}(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}\), the flow is \(\phi_t(\mathbf{x}) = e^{t\mathbf{A}}\mathbf{x}\).

Worked example — rotation in \(\mathbb{R}^2\): Generator \(X = -y\,\partial_x + x\,\partial_y\). Flow: \[ \phi_t(x,y) = (x\cos t - y\sin t,\; x\sin t + y\cos t) \] This is rotation through angle \(t\) — integrating the infinitesimal generator recovers \(SO(2)\).
Worked example — translation: \(X = \partial_x\) generates \(T_\varepsilon : (x,y) \mapsto (x+\varepsilon, y)\).

3) Lie bracket and commutator of flows

The Lie bracket of vector fields \(\mathbf{X}\) and \(\mathbf{Y}\) is \[ [\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}] = \mathbf{X}\mathbf{Y} - \mathbf{Y}\mathbf{X} \] (as differential operators on functions). It measures failure of flows to commute: \(\phi_s^{\mathbf{X}} \circ \phi_t^{\mathbf{Y}} \neq \phi_t^{\mathbf{Y}} \circ \phi_s^{\mathbf{X}}\) in general.

Worked example: On \(\mathbb{R}^2\), \(\mathbf{X} = \partial_x\), \(\mathbf{Y} = x\partial_y\). \([\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}] = \partial_y \neq 0\). Translations and shear do not commute.
Lie algebra: The space of infinitesimal symmetries of a system, closed under bracket, forms a Lie algebra — the infinitesimal version of a Lie group.

Related: E7 Lie Groups, Group Theory.

4) Prolongation and symmetry of ODEs

A vector field \(\mathbf{X}\) on the \((x,y)\)-plane prolongs to jet space to act on derivatives. For a first-order ODE \(F(x, y, y') = 0\), \(\mathbf{X}\) is a symmetry if the ODE is invariant under the flow of \(\mathbf{X}\) (prolonged action maps solutions to solutions).

Determining equations: Substitute the infinitesimal conditions (Lie symmetry conditions) into the invariance of \(F=0\). Solve for \(\xi(x,y)\), \(\eta(x,y)\) defining \(\mathbf{X} = \xi\partial_x + \eta\partial_y\).
Worked example: For \(y' = f(x)\) (no explicit \(y\)), translation in \(y\): \(\mathbf{X} = \partial_y\) is always a symmetry. Reduction of order follows from canonical coordinates for the symmetry.
Convention: Prolongation formulas depend on order of the equation. Second-order ODEs require prolongation to \((x, y, y', y'')\) variables.

5) Symmetry of PDEs and Noether's theorem

For a Lagrangian density \(\mathcal{L}(x, u, u_\mu)\) governing a PDE for field \(u\), an infinitesimal transformation \(\delta x\), \(\delta u\) leaves the action invariant if the variational symmetry condition holds. Noether's theorem yields conserved currents.

Energy conservation: Time translation \(\mathbf{X} = \partial_t\) preserves Lagrangians independent of \(t\); the conserved quantity is the Hamiltonian (energy).
Scale symmetry: \(\mathbf{X} = x\partial_x + y\partial_y\) (dilation) can yield conserved quantities when the action is scale-invariant — relevant in critical phenomena and fluid self-similarity.

Related: Calculus of Variations.

6) Lie groups and their algebras

A Lie group \(G\) is a smooth manifold with group structure. The Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g} = T_e G\) is identified with left-invariant vector fields; the bracket is the commutator. Exponential map \(\exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G\) integrates infinitesimal generators.

7) Applications and research directions

Integrable systems

Infinite-dimensional symmetry algebras (Kac–Moody) characterise integrable PDEs such as KdV and nonlinear Schrödinger equations.

Gauge theories

Local gauge symmetries are infinite-dimensional Lie algebras; BRST formalism uses infinitesimal gauge transformations with ghost fields.

Numerical integration

Geometric integrators preserve Lie group structure (symplectic, energy-preserving) by respecting infinitesimal symmetries of Hamiltonian flows.

Computer algebra

Packages (e.g. Lie symmetry routines) automate determining equations for PDE/ODE symmetry algebras — essential in applied mathematics research.

Related: Classical Mechanics, Groupoids.
