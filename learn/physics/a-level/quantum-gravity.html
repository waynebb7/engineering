1) The problem of quantum gravity

Quantum gravity (QG) is the quest for a theory that describes spacetime and gravitation consistently with quantum mechanics. We have two extraordinarily successful frameworks:

  • General relativity (GR) — gravity as curved spacetime geometry, classical and deterministic
  • Quantum field theory (QFT) — matter and forces on a fixed background spacetime

Neither suffices alone. GR predicts its own breakdown inside black holes and near the Big Bang singularity; QFT on curved backgrounds works only when gravity stays weak. A full QG theory must explain what spacetime itself is at the smallest scales.

2) Why GR and QFT clash

Perturbative quantization of GR — treating \(g_{\mu\nu} = \eta_{\mu\nu} + h_{\mu\nu}\) as a QFT on flat space — fails. The coupling has mass dimension \(-1\), so loop corrections generate infinitely many counterterms: the theory is non-renormalizable.

At the Planck scale (\(\ell_P \sim 10^{-35}\,\mathrm{m}\), \(t_P \sim 10^{-43}\,\mathrm{s}\), \(m_P \sim 10^{19}\,\mathrm{GeV}\)), quantum fluctuations of the metric itself become large. Classical pictures of smooth geometry break down.

Conceptual tension

GR is background-independent (no fixed stage); canonical QFT assumes a fixed spacetime. Time in GR is dynamical, complicating the Hamiltonian structure of quantum theory.

Information puzzles

Black-hole evaporation (Hawking radiation) suggests entropy \(S = A/(4\ell_P^2)\) and raises the black-hole information paradox — a sharp test for any QG proposal.

3) Semiclassical gravity

Where curvature is small but not negligible, semiclassical gravity treats spacetime classically and matter quantum mechanically. The semiclassical Einstein equation is:

\[G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\langle\hat T_{\mu\nu}\rangle\]

The expectation value of the stress–energy tensor sources classical curvature. This framework:

  • Derives Hawking radiation — black holes emit thermal spectra at temperature \(T_H = \hbar c^3/(8\pi G M k_B)\)
  • Explains gravitational particle production in expanding universes (cosmological particle creation)
  • Breaks down near singularities and the Planck scale — a full QG theory is still required

Cosmological context: Cosmology, Black Hole Physics.

4) String theory

String theory replaces point particles with one-dimensional extended objects (strings). The graviton emerges as a vibrational mode of the closed string — gravity is built in, not added.

  • Consistency: quantum strings require extra dimensions and fixed critical dimension (\(D=26\) bosonic, \(D=10\) superstring)
  • Unification: gauge bosons and fermions arise from open and closed string modes on D-branes and compact spaces
  • UV finiteness: extended objects soften short-distance divergences that plague point-particle QG
  • Landscape: many vacua complicate unique predictions; AdS/CFT provides non-perturbative dualities
Deeper dive: String Theory · Black Hole Physics.

5) Loop quantum gravity

Loop quantum gravity (LQG) quantizes GR directly without adding new particles or extra dimensions. It is background-independent: geometry itself is quantized.

  • Ashtekar variables: use spin-connection and densitized triad; build loop and spin-network states
  • Area and volume spectra are discrete — minimal length on the order of \(\ell_P\)
  • Spin foams provide a sum-over-histories path integral for quantum geometry
  • Cosmology: loop quantum cosmology replaces the Big Bang singularity with a bounce in some models

LQG does not automatically unify other forces (unlike string theory) but stays close to established four-dimensional GR. Matter coupling and recovery of classical GR in the continuum limit remain active research areas.

See also Loop Quantum Gravity.

6) Asymptotic safety

Asymptotic safety asks whether Einstein–Hilbert gravity could be a valid QFT in the ultraviolet if the renormalization group flow approaches a non-trivial fixed point as energy \(\to \infty\).

\[\beta(g_i)\big|_{g=g^*} = 0, \quad \text{with UV-attractive directions}\]

If so, only finitely many couplings are relevant — the theory is predictive despite being perturbatively non-renormalizable. Functional RG calculations find evidence for such fixed points in truncated approximations, but a complete proof is open.

Appeal

Minimalist — no strings, no extra dimensions. Gravity remains a metric theory, compatible with low-energy GR tests.

Challenge

Truncations may miss operators; connecting to observations (e.g. graviton scattering amplitudes, black-hole interiors) requires non-perturbative control.

7) Other approaches and observational hooks

Beyond the three main programmes, researchers pursue causal dynamical triangulations, group field theory, non-commutative geometry, and emergent gravity (thermodynamic or entropic spacetime). No experiment has directly probed \(\ell_P\), but indirect tests exist:

  • Gravitational-wave astronomy — ringdown spectra, propagation speed of GWs (LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA)
  • Cosmic microwave background — imprints of early-universe quantum gravity (speculative)
  • Tabletop gravity — proposed interferometric tests of superposition of mesoscopic masses
  • Black-hole observations — Event Horizon Telescope shadows vs QG-corrected metrics

Related: Early Universe, Computational Relativity.
