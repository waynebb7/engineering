1) Qubits as information carriers

In quantum information theory, a qubit is the fundamental unit of quantum information. Its state \(|\psi\rangle = \alpha|0\rangle + \beta|1\rangle\) carries two complex amplitudes subject to the normalisation constraint \(|\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1\).

A measurement in the computational basis extracts at most one classical bit — outcome 0 with probability \(|\alpha|^2\) or outcome 1 with probability \(|\beta|^2\). The amplitudes themselves are not directly accessible; quantum algorithms exploit interference among them before measurement.

Key idea: A qubit is not a probabilistic classical bit. Superposition and phase are physical resources that enable tasks impossible with classical information alone.

Foundations: Qubits and the Bloch Sphere, Dirac Notation, Hilbert Spaces.

2) Classical vs quantum information

Classical information is encoded in distinguishable states (bits, symbols). Quantum information generalises this to density operators on a Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\). For \(n\) qubits, the state space has dimension \(2^n\), but reading out all \(2^n\) amplitudes requires exponentially many measurements.

3) Von Neumann entropy

Uncertainty in a quantum state is quantified by the von Neumann entropy. For a density matrix \(\rho\) with eigenvalues \(\lambda_i\): \[ S(\rho) = -\mathrm{Tr}(\rho \log_2 \rho) = -\sum_i \lambda_i \log_2 \lambda_i \] Pure states have \(S = 0\); maximally mixed single-qubit states \(\rho = I/2\) have \(S = 1\) bit.

Entropy is invariant under unitary evolution: \(S(U\rho U^\dagger) = S(\rho)\). It can increase under open-system dynamics (noise, measurement, entanglement with an environment).

Physical meaning: \(S(\rho)\) measures the irreducible randomness in the outcomes of optimal measurements on \(\rho\). It generalises Shannon entropy to non-commuting observables.

Mixed states: Density Matrices; probability: Statistics and Probability.

4) Entanglement entropy

For a bipartite pure state \(|\psi\rangle_{AB}\), the entanglement entropy of subsystem \(A\) is the von Neumann entropy of its reduced density matrix: \[ S_A = S(\rho_A), \quad \rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|) \] Product states have \(S_A = 0\); maximally entangled two-qubit states (e.g. Bell states) have \(S_A = 1\).

For the Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle + |11\rangle)\): \[ \rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(|\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|) = \frac{1}{2}(|0\rangle\langle 0| + |1\rangle\langle 1|) \] so \(S_A = 1\) bit — subsystem \(A\) alone looks completely mixed even though the joint state is pure.

Area law (preview): In many-body ground states of local Hamiltonians, entanglement entropy across a spatial cut often scales with the boundary area, not the volume — a deep link to holography and tensor networks.

Entanglement foundations: Entanglement and Bell Inequalities, Multi-Qubit Systems.

5) Quantum mutual information

Classical mutual information \(I(A:B) = H(A) + H(B) - H(AB)\) measures shared randomness. The quantum version uses von Neumann entropies: \[ I(A:B) = S(\rho_A) + S(\rho_B) - S(\rho_{AB}) \] For pure bipartite states, \(S(\rho_{AB}) = 0\) so \(I(A:B) = 2 S_A\) — entanglement saturates the quantum mutual information.

Quantum mutual information underpins Quantum Teleportation and capacity theorems for quantum channels. It can exceed what is possible with classical correlations alone.

Nuance: Entanglement is not the only form of quantum correlation. Some separable mixed states still exhibit quantum discord — correlations beyond classical mutual information.

6) Quantum channels — preview

When a quantum system interacts with an environment, its evolution is a quantum channel \(\mathcal{E}\): a completely positive, trace-preserving (CPTP) map on density matrices. Every channel admits a Kraus representation: \[ \mathcal{E}(\rho) = \sum_k E_k \rho E_k^\dagger, \quad \sum_k E_k^\dagger E_k = I \]

Common noise models on a single qubit:

  • Depolarising: \(\mathcal{E}(\rho) = (1-p)\rho + \frac{p}{3}(X\rho X + Y\rho Y + Z\rho Z)\)
  • Amplitude damping: energy dissipation \(|1\rangle \to |0\rangle\)
  • Phase damping: loss of off-diagonal coherence without energy change
Channel capacity: The maximum rate at which classical or quantum information can be transmitted reliably through \(\mathcal{E}\) defines fundamental limits on quantum communication and fault-tolerant thresholds.

Full treatment: Quantum Channels and Decoherence.

7) Link to quantum computing

Quantum information theory provides the language for quantum computing: qubits are manipulated by unitary gates, entanglement enables multi-qubit algorithms, and channels model the noise that Quantum Error Correction must overcome.

Algorithms

Entanglement and interference power Grover, Shor, and variational methods like VQE.

Hardware

Real devices are open systems. Coherence times, gate fidelities, and readout errors are all channel-theoretic quantities on physical qubits.

Start computing: Quantum Circuits, Quantum Gates, Algorithms Overview, NISQ Era Computing.
