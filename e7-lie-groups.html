E7 Lie Groups

Postgraduate guide. The exceptional Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{e}_7\), root systems, Dynkin diagrams, \(\mathrm{E}_7\) groups, and representation theory.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Simple Lie algebras and the exceptional series

Finite-dimensional simple Lie algebras over \(\mathbb{C}\) are classified as types \(A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n\) and the five exceptional types \(E_6, E_7, E_8, F_4, G_2\). The Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) has dimension 133 and rank 7.

Key idea: Exceptional Lie algebras do not belong to infinite classical families; they arise from unique root systems and encode symmetries appearing in string theory, Jordan algebras, and arithmetic geometry.

Prerequisite: Lie Algebras and Group Theory.

2) Root system of type \(E_7\)

A root system \(\Phi \subset \mathbb{R}^7\) for \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) has \(|\Phi| = 126\) roots. One construction embeds \(E_7\) in \(E_8\): take roots of \(E_8\) orthogonal to a fixed root \(\alpha\).

Explicit roots: In the \(E_8\) coordinate model, \(E_7\) roots are integer vectors of norm \(\sqrt{2}\) in the hyperplane \(\alpha^\perp\). There are 126 such roots forming two \(\mathrm{W}(E_7)\)-orbits of lengths 70 and 56 in some conventions.
Simple roots: A choice of positive roots yields 7 simple roots \(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_7\) with Cartan matrix encoding inner products: \[ A_{ij} = 2\frac{(\alpha_i, \alpha_j)}{(\alpha_i, \alpha_i)} \]

4) The Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) and its structure

\(\mathfrak{e}_7 = \mathfrak{h} \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Phi} \mathfrak{g}_\alpha\) with Cartan subalgebra \(\mathfrak{h} \cong \mathbb{C}^7\). The Chevalley–Serre relations determined by the Cartan matrix define \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) completely.

Centre and Killing form: \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) is simple, so the Killing form is nondegenerate. The Casimir element \(\Omega \in U(\mathfrak{e}_7)\) acts as a scalar on each irreducible representation.
Jordan algebra connection: The 27-dimensional exceptional Jordan algebra \(J_{3,\mathbb{O}}\) has automorphism group \(F_4\); the \(E_7\) structure appears in the Freudenthal construction and cubic norm geometry.

5) The Lie group \(\mathrm{E}_7\)

A simply connected complex Lie group \(G\) with \(\mathrm{Lie}(G) = \mathfrak{e}_7\) is denoted \(\mathrm{E}_7\). The compact real form \(\mathrm{E}_7^{\mathrm{comp}}\) has dimension 133 and is simply connected.

Root lattice: The weight lattice \(P(E_7)\) and coroot lattice \(Q^\vee\) control representation integrality. The fundamental dominant weights \(\omega_i\) label finite-dimensional irreducible modules \(L(\omega_i)\).
Minimal representation: The smallest nontrivial faithful representation of \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) has dimension 56 (the "minuscule" representation associated to the short leg of the \(E_7\) Dynkin diagram).

Related: Clifford Algebra and Tensors.

6) Weyl group and character theory

The Weyl group \(W(E_7) = \langle s_{\alpha_i}\rangle\) acts on \(\mathfrak{h}^*\). It has order \(|W(E_7)| = 290\,304 = 2^{14} \cdot 3^2 \cdot 5 \cdot 7\).

Weyl character formula: For dominant weight \(\lambda\), \[ \mathrm{ch}\,L(\lambda) = \frac{\sum_{w \in W} \varepsilon(w)\,e^{w(\lambda + \rho)}}{\sum_{w \in W} \varepsilon(w)\,e^{w\rho}} \] where \(\rho\) is half the sum of positive roots.

Branching rules

Restricting \(\mathrm{E}_7\) representations to maximal subgroups (\(\mathrm{E}_6\), \(\mathrm{SU}(8)\), etc.) decomposes modules for physics compactifications.

Weight multiplicities

Kostant partition functions and Weyl dimension formulas compute multiplicities algorithmically.

7) Applications

String theory and M-theory

\(\mathrm{E}_7\) appears in compactifications of 11-dimensional supergravity; the 56-dimensional representation organises charge vectors.

Arithmetic geometry

Period domains for K3 surfaces and certain Calabi–Yau families carry \(\mathrm{E}_7\) (or related) symmetry actions.

Exceptional objects

Freudenthal–Tits magic square links \(E_7\) to octonionic projective planes and exceptional Jordan algebras.

Representation stability

Understanding \(\mathrm{E}_7\) modules informs branching in classical series limits and Lie-theoretic machine learning feature design.

8) Quick knowledge check

  1. What are the dimension and rank of \(\mathfrak{e}_7\)?
  2. How many roots does the \(E_7\) root system have?
  3. Draw (or describe) the \(E_7\) Dynkin diagram.
  4. What is the order of the Weyl group \(W(E_7)\)?
  5. State the dimension of the minimal nontrivial representation of \(\mathfrak{e}_7\).
  6. Define the Cartan matrix entry \(A_{ij}\).
  7. Name one real or complex form of the \(\mathrm{E}_7\) Lie group.

For marking: use root-system language consistently, cite dominant weights for representations, and distinguish algebra \(\mathfrak{e}_7\) from group \(\mathrm{E}_7\).
