A-Level guide. SOHCAHTOA, sine and cosine rules, area \(\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C\), and bearings.

1) Trigonometry in right-angled triangles

Trigonometry relates angles and sides. For a right-angled triangle, label the sides relative to angle \(\theta\): Opposite, Adjacent, and Hypotenuse.

SOHCAHTOA:
\(\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}}\)   \(\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}}\)   \(\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Adj}}\)

Finding a side

Choose the ratio linking known side and unknown side with \(\theta\).

Finding an angle

Use \(\sin^{-1}\), \(\cos^{-1}\), or \(\tan^{-1}\) on the calculator.

Builds on Geometry (Pythagoras) and leads to Trig Functions and the Unit Circle.

2) SOHCAHTOA — worked examples

Find a side: In a right triangle, \(\theta = 35°\), hypotenuse 12 cm. Find the opposite side.
\(\sin 35° = \dfrac{\text{opp}}{12}\) → opp \(= 12\sin 35° \approx 6.88\) cm.
Find an angle: Opposite 5 cm, adjacent 12 cm.
\(\tan\theta = \tfrac{5}{12}\) → \(\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\tfrac{5}{12}\right) \approx 22.6°\).
Common mistake: Using the wrong ratio — check which sides are opposite and adjacent to your chosen angle, not another angle in the triangle.

3) The sine rule

For any triangle with sides \(a, b, c\) opposite angles \(A, B, C\): \[\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}\]

Find a side

Use when you have a side–angle pair and another angle.

Find an angle

Use when you know two sides and a non-included angle (ambiguous case possible).

Worked example: Triangle with \(A = 40°\), \(B = 60°\), side \(a = 10\) cm. Find \(b\).
\(\dfrac{b}{\sin 60°} = \dfrac{10}{\sin 40°}\) → \(b = \dfrac{10\sin 60°}{\sin 40°} \approx 13.5\) cm.

4) The cosine rule

When you have two sides and the included angle, or all three sides:

Cosine rule:
\(a^2 = b^2 + c^2 − 2bc\cos A\)
\(\cos A = \dfrac{b^2 + c^2 − a^2}{2bc}\)
Worked example: Sides \(b = 7\) cm, \(c = 9\) cm, included angle \(A = 120°\). Find \(a\).
\(a^2 = 49 + 81 − 2(7)(9)\cos 120° = 130 − 126(−0.5) = 193\)
\(a = \sqrt{193} \approx 13.9\) cm.
Choosing a rule: Right angle → SOHCAHTOA. Two angles and a side → sine rule. Two sides + included angle, or three sides → cosine rule.

5) Area — \(\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C\)

The area of a triangle with sides \(a\) and \(b\) and included angle \(C\) is: \[\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C\]

Worked example: \(a = 8\) cm, \(b = 11\) cm, \(C = 50°\).
Area \(= \tfrac{1}{2}(8)(11)\sin 50° = 44\sin 50° \approx 33.7\) cm².

Heron's formula (extension)

With three sides: use semi-perimeter \(s = \tfrac{a+b+c}{2}\), Area \(= \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}\).

Link to geometry

6) Bearings

A bearing is a direction measured clockwise from North, written as three figures (e.g. \(045°\), \(127°\)).

Strategy:
1. Draw a North line at the starting point.
2. Measure clockwise to the direction of travel.
3. Use angle facts and trigonometry to find distances.
Worked example: Ship sails 10 km on bearing \(060°\), then 8 km on bearing \(150°\). Sketch both legs from a common start; use cosine rule on the triangle formed by the two legs and the return line to find distance from start.
Direction Bearing
Due East \(090°\)
Due South \(180°\)
Due West \(270°\)
North-East \(045°\)
