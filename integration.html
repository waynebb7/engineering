Integration in Electrical Engineering

1. Capacitors and Inductors

The behavior of capacitors and inductors involves time integrals:

  • Capacitor voltage: \( V_C(t) = \frac{1}{C} \int i(t)\,dt + V_0 \)
  • Inductor current: \( i_L(t) = \frac{1}{L} \int v(t)\,dt + i_0 \)

2. Energy Calculations

Electrical energy is obtained by integrating power:

\( E = \int P(t)\,dt \)

Capacitor and inductor stored energy: \[ E_C = \frac{1}{2}CV^2, \quad E_L = \frac{1}{2}LI^2 \]

3. Signal Processing

Integration is used in:

  • Analog integrators (e.g., op-amp circuits): \( V_{out} = -\frac{1}{RC} \int V_{in}(t)\,dt \)
  • Smoothing signals and low-pass filtering

4. Control Systems

In PID controllers, the integral term reduces steady-state error:

\( u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t)\,dt + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} \)

5. Electromagnetics and Fields

Surface and volume integrals are fundamental in field theory:

  • Gauss’s Law: \[ \oint_S \vec{D} \cdot d\vec{A} = Q_{\text{enclosed}} \]
  • Faraday’s Law (integral form): \[ \oint_C \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

6. System Modeling & Simulation

System equations often include integrals. Example (RLC circuit):

\( L \frac{d^2i}{dt^2} + R \frac{di}{dt} + \frac{1}{C} i(t) = V(t) \)

Simulation tools (e.g., SPICE, MATLAB) use numerical integration to solve circuit behavior over time.

7. Embedded & Real-Time Systems

Microcontrollers integrate sensor data to compute:

  • Velocity from acceleration
  • Position from velocity

8. Power Systems

Energy metering involves integration of instantaneous power: \[ E = \int_0^T P(t)\,dt \]

This is how utility meters calculate energy usage.
