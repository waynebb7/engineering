Hodge Conjecture

Research guide. Hodge structures, algebraic cycles, cohomology of smooth projective varieties, and the Clay Millennium Problem relating topology to algebra.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Cohomology of smooth projective varieties

Let \(X\) be a smooth projective variety over \(\mathbb{C}\) of complex dimension \(n\). The singular cohomology \(H^k(X, \mathbb{Q})\) is a finite-dimensional \(\mathbb{Q}\)-vector space. Poincaré duality gives \(H^k(X, \mathbb{Q}) \cong H^{2n-k}(X, \mathbb{Q})^*\).

De Rham's theorem identifies \(H^k(X, \mathbb{C}) \cong H^k_{\mathrm{dR}}(X)\), the de Rham cohomology of holomorphic differential forms. Hodge theory decomposes the cohomology according to complex structure.

Hodge decomposition: \[ H^k(X, \mathbb{C}) = \bigoplus_{p+q=k} H^{p,q}(X), \] where \(H^{p,q}(X)\) consists of cohomology classes represented by \((p,q)\)-forms. Complex conjugation exchanges \(H^{p,q}\) and \(H^{q,p}\).

Prerequisite: Algebraic Geometry, Differential Geometry, and Topology.

2) Hodge structures and the Hodge diamond

The Hodge numbers \(h^{p,q} = \dim H^{p,q}(X)\) arrange into a Hodge diamond. For a Kähler manifold, \(h^{p,q} = h^{q,p}\) and \(h^{p,q} = h^{n-p,n-q}\) (Hodge symmetry).

(p,p)-classes: The diagonal entries \(H^{p,p}(X) \cap H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Q})\) are the classes of greatest arithmetic interest. They are the natural home for classes of algebraic cycles.

A pure Hodge structure of weight \(k\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-vector space \(H\) with a decomposition \(H \otimes \mathbb{C} = \bigoplus_{p+q=k} H^{p,q}\) satisfying complex conjugation symmetry. Cohomology of smooth projective varieties carries such structures.

Example Hodge diamond feature
Curve of genus \(g\) \(h^{1,0} = h^{0,1} = g\)
Abelian variety Hodge structure determined by period matrix
Complete intersection Hodge numbers computable from degree data

3) Algebraic cycles

An algebraic cycle of codimension \(p\) on \(X\) is a formal \(\mathbb{Z}\)-linear combination of irreducible subvarieties of codimension \(p\). The cycle class map \[ \mathrm{cl} : \mathrm{CH}^p(X) \to H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Z}) \] sends a cycle to its cohomology class (Poincaré dual to the fundamental class).

Image lies in (p,p): Algebraic cycle classes always lie in \(H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Q}) \cap H^{p,p}(X)\). These are Hodge classes (sometimes called analytic Hodge classes when defined via currents).

The Chow group \(\mathrm{CH}^p(X)\) modulo rational equivalence is the algebraic cycle group. The Hodge conjecture asks whether every Hodge class is algebraic — i.e. lies in the image of \(\mathrm{cl} \otimes \mathbb{Q}\).

4) Statement of the Hodge Conjecture

Hodge Conjecture (Millennium Problem): Let \(X\) be a smooth projective variety over \(\mathbb{C}\). Every class \(\alpha \in H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Q}) \cap H^{p,p}(X)\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-linear combination of classes of algebraic cycles of codimension \(p\).

Equivalently: the cycle class map \(\mathrm{CH}^p(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q} \to H^{2p}(X, \mathbb{Q}) \cap H^{p,p}(X)\) is surjective.

The conjecture bridges topology (Hodge decomposition from the Kähler metric) and algebra (subvarieties and polynomial equations). It asserts that Hodge theory detects algebraic geometry completely on the \((p,p)\) diagonal.

Integral vs rational: The rational version (with \(\mathbb{Q}\) coefficients) is the Millennium Problem. The integral Hodge conjecture is false: Atiyah–Hirzebruch gave counterexamples. The rational version remains open.

5) Known cases and partial results

Low codimension

\(p = 1\) (divisors): True by the Lefschetz (1,1) theorem: every (1,1)-class is the cohomology class of a divisor. This is the foundational case.

Abelian varieties

The Hodge conjecture holds for abelian varieties (Fulton–Harris, Mumford). Hodge classes on abelian varieties are \(\mathbb{Q}\)-linear combinations of classes of abelian subvarieties.

Special varieties

Proved for products of curves (Fulton), certain complete intersections, and varieties with \(h^{p,q} = 0\) for \(p \neq q\) on the relevant diagonal.

Function field analogues

Over finite fields, the Tate conjecture is the étale analogue. Deligne proved it for varieties over finite fields in many cases; the Hodge conjecture over \(\mathbb{C}\) is harder because transcendental methods dominate.

Motives: Grothendieck's theory of motives provides a universal cohomology theory. The Hodge conjecture is equivalent to the statement that the Hodge realization of the motive is surjective onto Hodge classes. See Projective Variety.

6) Obstacles and generalizations

Several related conjectures sharpen or generalize the problem:

  • Standard conjectures (Grothendieck): Lefschetz and Künneth-type statements for cycle classes. If proved, they would imply the Hodge conjecture in many settings.
  • Hodge conjecture for motives: Formulated in the category of pure motives; would unify étale, de Rham, and Betti cohomology.
  • Generalized Hodge conjecture: Relates Hodge filtrations on Chow groups to the conjecture (Bloch, Beilinson).
Counterexamples to variants: The integral Hodge conjecture fails (Atiyah–Hirzebruch). The Hodge conjecture for Kähler manifolds that are not projective is open and may fail; projectivity is essential to the Millennium formulation.
Status (2025): The Hodge Conjecture remains open. It is one of seven Clay Millennium Prize Problems with a \$1M bounty.

7) Research connections

Arithmetic geometry

The Tate conjecture over finite fields and the Hodge conjecture over \(\mathbb{C}\) are parallel. Results in Algebraic Number Theory inform both.

Mirror symmetry

String theory and mirror symmetry relate Hodge structures on mirror pairs of Calabi–Yau manifolds, providing heuristic support and computational tools.

Related: Algebraic Geometry, Projective Variety, Millennium Prize Problems.
