1. Rate of Change of Current and Voltage

For inductors and capacitors, voltage or current depends on derivatives:
\( V_L = L \frac{dI}{dt} \),   \( I_C = C \frac{dV}{dt} \)

2. Signal Analysis

  • Finding instantaneous rates of change in voltage or current
  • Derivative operations in filters and signal processing

3. Control Systems

The derivative term in PID control enhances system response: \[ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t) dt + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} \]

4. Transient Analysis

Describing how circuits respond to sudden changes requires differential equations.

5. Electromagnetics

Maxwell‚Äôs equations involve derivatives of fields: \[ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = - \frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \]

6. Power and Energy Rates

Power \( P = \frac{dE}{dt} \), rate of energy transfer or consumption.

7. Waveforms and Oscillations

Differentiation describes changes in AC signals and waveforms.

1. Flight Dynamics

Velocity and acceleration are derivatives of position: \[ v(t) = \frac{ds(t)}{dt}, \quad a(t) = \frac{dv(t)}{dt} \]

2. Aerodynamics

Change rates of lift/drag coefficients with respect to angle of attack: \[ \frac{dC_L}{d\alpha}, \quad \frac{dC_D}{d\alpha} \]

3. Structural Dynamics

Strain as spatial derivative of displacement: \[ \varepsilon = \frac{du}{dx} \] Vibration frequencies are related to derivatives of displacement.

4. Control Systems

Derivative control term in flight control: \[ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t) dt + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} \]

5. Propulsion

Engine RPM changes as derivative of rotational position: \[ \frac{d\theta}{dt} = \omega \]

6. Navigation

Angular velocity and rate of change of orientation from gyroscopes.

7. Flight Simulation

Equations of motion involve derivatives of velocity and position.

8. Instruments

Vertical Speed Indicator measures \( \frac{dh}{dt} \), rate of climb or descent.
