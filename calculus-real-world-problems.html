Real-World Applications of Calculus

1. Engineering – Suspension Bridge Design

Calculating the bending moment for a uniformly distributed load:

M(x) = (w/2) * x * (L - x)

Used to determine structural load and stress points.

2. Physics – Spacecraft Landing on Mars

Using integration to calculate velocity and position:

v(t) = -3.71t + v₀
s(t) = -1.855t² + v₀t + s₀

Models motion under Martian gravity.

3. Economics – Profit Maximization

Find maximum profit where marginal cost equals marginal revenue:

P(x) = -2x² + 100x - 500
P'(x) = -4x + 100 → x = 25

Calculus helps identify optimal production levels.

4. Environmental Science – Rainwater Runoff

Total rainwater accumulation over time:

r(t) = 5t
∫₀² 5t dt = 10 liters

Used to forecast flood levels and drainage needs.

5. Computer Science – Simulating Object Movement

Physics engine using calculus:

a(t) = -9.8
v(t) = -9.8t + v₀
s(t) = -4.9t² + v₀t + s₀

Simulates realistic motion in games or simulations.

6. Biology/Medicine – Drug Absorption

Modeling exponential decay of drug concentration:

C(t) = C₀ * e^(-kt)
AUC = ∫₀^∞ C₀ * e^(-kt) dt = C₀ / k

Calculates total exposure to a drug over time.
