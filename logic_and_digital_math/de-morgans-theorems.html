De Morgan's Theorems

Digital logic guide. Both De Morgan laws, negating compound expressions, and equivalent gate transformations.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Why De Morgan matters

Augustus De Morgan's theorems describe how negation distributes over AND and OR. In digital design they let you convert between NAND/NOR implementations and rewrite expressions for fewer gate types or simpler wiring.

The two laws:
(1) (A · B)' = A' + B' — NOT of AND equals OR of NOTs
(2) (A + B)' = A' · B' — NOT of OR equals AND of NOTs

Prerequisites: Boolean Algebra and Truth Tables.

2) Law 1 — NOT of AND

Break the bar: complement each input, change AND to OR.

A B AB (AB)' A' B' A' + B'
0001111
0101101
1001011
1110000
Columns (AB)' and A' + B' match — the law holds for all inputs.

3) Law 2 — NOT of OR

Complement each input, change OR to AND.

A B A + B (A + B)' A' B' A' · B'
0001111
0110100
1010010
1110000

Gate view

NOR gate output = (A + B)' = A' · B' when built from AND + inverters

Dual relationship

AND ↔ OR swap under negation — a duality principle in Boolean algebra.

4) Extended forms (many variables)

De Morgan generalises to any number of variables:

  • (ABC)' = A' + B' + C'
  • (A + B + C)' = A' · B' · C'
Worked example: (AB + CD)'
Treat (AB) and (CD) as single terms: (X + Y)' = X' · Y' where X=AB, Y=CD
= (AB)' · (CD)' = (A' + B')(C' + D')
Common mistake: Forgetting to complement every term inside the parentheses. (A + B)' is not A' + B.

5) Gate transformations

De Morgan converts between positive-logic AND/OR networks and NAND/NOR equivalents — useful when a chip provides only one gate type.

AND from NAND + NOT

NAND followed by inverter = AND

By De Morgan: double negation restores AND

OR from NAND only

A OR B = (A' · B')' — NAND of inverted inputs

NAND completeness: Any function expressible with AND/OR/NOT can be rebuilt with NAND alone — Truth Functional Completeness.

6) Algebraic simplification examples

Example 1: Simplify F = (A'B)'
De Morgan: A'' + B' = A + B'
Example 2: Convert F = ((A + B)(C + D))'
Let P = (A + B) and Q = (C + D). Then F = (PQ)' = P' + Q' by De Morgan on the product.
= (A + B)' + (C + D)' = A'B' + C'D'

Verify each step on a Truth Table when learning. Combine with other laws from Boolean Algebra.

7) Engineering applications

  • CMOS/NMOS — NAND and NOR are often faster or denser than AND/OR
  • Active-low signals — bar notation on pin names; De Morgan rewrites active-high logic
  • Code optimisation — compiler logic passes apply similar complement rules
  • Bus decoding — negated enable terms in Decoders
Link to arithmetic: Inverter chains appear in one's/two's complement paths — Binary Arithmetic.
