Beyond the Standard Model

Quantum guide. The Standard Model recap, evidence it is incomplete — neutrino masses, dark matter, hierarchy — and leading extensions including supersymmetry and dark-matter candidates.

← Quantum Subjects Catalog

1) Standard Model recap

The Standard Model (SM) is the gauge QFT of particle physics. Its gauge group is \(SU(3)_C \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y\), spontaneously broken to \(SU(3)_C \times U(1)_{\mathrm{EM}}\) by the Higgs field acquiring a vacuum expectation value \(v \approx 246\,\mathrm{GeV}\).

  • Matter fermions — three generations of quarks and leptons (\(e,\mu,\tau\); \(u,c,t\); etc.)
  • Gauge bosons — gluons (8), \(W^\pm\), \(Z^0\), photon \(\gamma\)
  • Higgs boson — scalar \(H\), discovered at the LHC (2012), mass \(\sim 125\,\mathrm{GeV}\)

The SM is renormalizable and predicts cross sections and decay rates to extraordinary precision. All accelerator experiments to date are consistent with it — yet it is widely regarded as an effective theory, not the final word.

Foundations: Quantum Field Theory, Nuclear and Particle Physics.

2) What the SM misses

Several observed phenomena lie outside the minimal SM:

  • Neutrino masses — oscillation experiments prove neutrinos are massive and mix
  • Dark matter — \(\sim 27\%\) of the universe's energy density; no SM candidate
  • Dark energy — cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) scale utterly unexplained
  • Matter–antimatter asymmetry — baryogenesis requires beyond-SM CP violation and out-of-equilibrium processes
  • Gravity — not included; see Quantum Gravity

Structural puzzles

Why three generations? Why these masses and mixings? The pattern suggests a deeper theory at higher energy.

Theoretical unease

Quadratic sensitivity of the Higgs mass to UV physics (hierarchy problem); instability of the electroweak vacuum in pure SM (depends on \(m_H\), \(m_t\)).

3) Neutrino masses and mixing

The minimal SM has massless neutrinos. Oscillation data (Super-Kamiokande, SNO, reactor and accelerator experiments) requires mass splittings and a Pontecorvo–Maki–Nakagawa–Sakata (PMNS) mixing matrix analogous to the quark CKM matrix:

\[\begin{pmatrix}\nu_e\\\nu_\mu\\\nu_\tau\end{pmatrix} = U_{\mathrm{PMNS}}\begin{pmatrix}\nu_1\\\nu_2\\\nu_3\end{pmatrix}\]

The seesaw mechanism adds heavy right-handed neutrinos \(N_R\):

\[\mathcal{L} \supset -y_\nu\,\bar L\,\tilde H\,N_R - \tfrac{1}{2}M_R\,\bar N_R^c N_R + \mathrm{h.c.}\]

After electroweak symmetry breaking, light left-handed neutrino masses scale as \(m_\nu \sim y_\nu^2 v^2/M_R\). Large \(M_R \sim 10^{14}\,\mathrm{GeV}\) naturally gives \(m_\nu \sim 0.1\,\mathrm{eV}\) and connects to leptogenesis (matter–antimatter asymmetry). Laboratory searches continue for neutrinoless double-beta decay, which would prove neutrinos are Majorana fermions.

4) The hierarchy problem

The Higgs mass receives quantum corrections \(\delta m_H^2 \propto \Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}}^2\) from virtual loops — quadratically sensitive to the cutoff \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}}\). For \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}} \sim m_P\), a bare counterterm fine-tuned to 30+ decimal places would be needed to keep \(m_H \approx 125\,\mathrm{GeV}\).

Proposed resolutions:

  • Supersymmetry — bosonic and fermionic loops cancel (if SUSY breaking scale \(\lesssim 1\,\mathrm{TeV}\))
  • Composite Higgs — Higgs is a bound state; no fundamental scalar at high energy
  • Extra dimensions — gravity spreads into bulk, diluting effective cutoff
  • Anthropic / multiverse — statistical selection (controversial, hard to test)
LHC context: No superpartners found up to \(\sim 2\,\mathrm{TeV}\) pushes many natural SUSY models to higher scales or alternative explanations.

5) Supersymmetry (SUSY)

Supersymmetry extends the Poincaré algebra with fermionic generators \(Q_\alpha\), relating bosons and fermions: every particle has a superpartner differing by spin \(\tfrac{1}{2}\).

  • Minimal Supersymmetric SM (MSSM) — doubles the particle spectrum; two Higgs doublets
  • Lightest superpartner (LSP) — if stable (R-parity conserved), a leading WIMP dark-matter candidate
  • Gauge coupling unification — MSSM couplings meet near \(10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}\)
  • Higgs mass — radiative corrections raise \(m_H\) above tree-level bound

SUSY must be broken at low energy (superpartners not yet discovered). Soft-breaking terms preserve renormalizability while hiding superpartners from current collider limits. Extended models (NMSSM, split SUSY, gauge mediation) explore different breaking patterns.

Mathematical structure: Groupoids · Lie algebras and representation theory.

6) Dark matter candidates

Astrophysical evidence (galaxy rotation curves, CMB, gravitational lensing) requires non-luminous matter. Leading BSM dark matter (DM) candidates:

WIMPs

Weakly interacting massive particles — thermally produced in the early universe with \(\langle\sigma v\rangle \sim 10^{-26}\,\mathrm{cm^3/s}\) ("WIMP miracle"). Examples: neutralino (SUSY LSP), heavy sterile neutrinos.

Axions

Pseudo-Goldstone bosons from Peccei–Quinn symmetry (strong CP problem). Very light (\(m_a \sim \mu\mathrm{eV}\)–meV), produced non-thermally; searched via haloscopes and helioscopes.

  • Sterile neutrinos — keV–GeV scale; X-ray line and structure-formation constraints
  • Primordial black holes — not strictly BSM but alternative DM explanation
  • Hidden-sector DM — feebly coupled sectors with portal interactions to the SM

Direct-detection experiments (LZ, XENON, PandaX) probe spin-independent WIMP–nucleon scattering; indirect searches look for annihilation products; colliders seek missing-energy signatures.

Cosmology: Cosmology.

7) Other BSM hints and programmes

Beyond the headline problems, several anomalies and frameworks drive BSM research:

  • Muon \(g-2\) — Brookhaven and Fermilab measurements differ from SM by \(\sim 2.5\sigma\); possible loops of new particles (leptoquarks, dark photons)
  • Flavor anomalies — \(B \to D^{(*)}\tau\nu\), \(b \to s\ell^+\ell^-\) tensions (evolving with data)
  • Grand unified theories (GUTs) — \(SU(5)\), \(SO(10)\) embed SM; predict proton decay (not yet seen)
  • Technicolor / composite models — dynamical EWSB without elementary Higgs
  • Two-Higgs-doublet models, extra gauge bosons \(Z'\) — model-independent search targets at colliders
