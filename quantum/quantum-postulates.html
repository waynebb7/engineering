Postulates of Quantum Mechanics

Quantum guide. The standard axioms — state space, observables, measurement, and evolution — that define non-relativistic quantum theory.

← Quantum Subjects Catalog

1) Postulate I: State space

The state of an isolated physical system is represented by a unit vector \(|\psi\rangle\) in a complex Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\). For composite systems, \(\mathcal{H} = \mathcal{H}_1 \otimes \mathcal{H}_2 \otimes \cdots\).

Ray interpretation: \(|\psi\rangle\) and \(e^{i\phi}|\psi\rangle\) describe the same physics — only the ray in \(\mathcal{H}\) matters. Notation: Dirac Notation.

2) Postulate II: Observables

Every measurable physical quantity (observable) corresponds to a Hermitian operator \(A = A^\dagger\) on \(\mathcal{H}\). Possible measurement outcomes are eigenvalues of \(A\); eigenstates \(|a_n\rangle\) satisfy \(A|a_n\rangle = a_n|a_n\rangle\).

Observable Operator (1D) Spectrum
Position \(\hat{x}\) Continuous \(\mathbb{R}\)
Momentum \(\hat{p} = -i\hbar\dfrac{d}{dx}\) Continuous \(\mathbb{R}\)
Energy Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}\) Discrete or continuous
Spin \(S_z\) \(\dfrac{\hbar}{2}\sigma_z\) \(\pm\hbar/2\)

Spin operators: Spin and Pauli Matrices.

3) Postulate III: Measurement

Upon measurement of \(A\) with outcome \(a_n\):

  1. Probability: \(P(a_n) = |\langle a_n|\psi\rangle|^2\) (Born rule)
  2. State update: \(|\psi\rangle \to |a_n\rangle\) (ideal projective measurement)

Expectation: \(\langle A \rangle = \langle\psi|A|\psi\rangle\). Full treatment of superposition and POVMs: Superposition and Measurement.

4) Postulate IV: Time evolution

Between measurements, an isolated system evolves unitarily:

\[i\hbar\frac{d}{dt}|\psi(t)\rangle = \hat{H}|\psi(t)\rangle\]

Formal solution: \(|\psi(t)\rangle = U(t)|\psi(0)\rangle\) where \(U(t) = e^{-i\hat{H}t/\hbar}\) (for time-independent \(\hat{H}\)). \(U\) is unitary: \(U^\dagger U = I\).

Stationary states: If \(\hat{H}|E_n\rangle = E_n|E_n\rangle\), then \(|E_n\rangle\) evolves only in phase: \(|E_n(t)\rangle = e^{-iE_n t/\hbar}|E_n\rangle\).

5) Composite systems and entanglement

The postulates apply to the full Hilbert space of composite systems. Entangled states violate classical factorisation of joint probabilities — a consequence of the tensor-product structure plus linearity.

Entanglement and Bell Inequalities show this empirically.

6) Mixed states extension

When a system is not in a pure state \(|\psi\rangle\) but a statistical ensemble, use a density operator \(\rho\):

  • \(\rho \geq 0\), \(\mathrm{Tr}(\rho) = 1\)
  • \(\langle A \rangle = \mathrm{Tr}(\rho A)\)
  • Pure state: \(\rho = |\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\)

Full theory: Density Matrices; open-system evolution: Quantum Channels.

7) Correspondence and limits

Classical mechanics emerges when \(\hbar\) is negligible relative to action scales, and when decoherence suppresses interference between macroscopically distinct alternatives. The Uncertainty Principle follows from the commutator \([\hat{x},\hat{p}] = i\hbar\).

Scope: These postulates describe non-relativistic quantum mechanics. Relativistic and field-theoretic extensions live in the Physics catalog (QFT, etc.).

8) Quick knowledge check

  1. State Postulate I in one sentence.
  2. What kind of operator represents an observable?
  3. Write the Born rule for eigenvalue \(a_n\).
  4. What is the Schrödinger equation?
  5. Why are only eigenvalues observed in ideal measurements?
  6. Express a pure-state density matrix.
  7. How does tensor product structure enable entanglement?

Answers: state = unit vector in Hilbert space; Hermitian; \(|\langle a_n|\psi\rangle|^2\); \(i\hbar\partial_t|\psi\rangle = \hat{H}|\psi\rangle\); collapse projects onto eigenstates; \(\rho=|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\); most vectors in \(\mathcal{H}_A\otimes\mathcal{H}_B\) are not product states.
