Multi-Qubit Systems

Quantum guide. Tensor products, composite state spaces, separable vs entangled states, and the exponential growth of Hilbert space dimension.

← Quantum Subjects Catalog

1) Composite Hilbert spaces

For qubits \(A\) and \(B\) with spaces \(\mathcal{H}_A \cong \mathbb{C}^2\) and \(\mathcal{H}_B \cong \mathbb{C}^2\), the joint system lives in the tensor product: \[ \mathcal{H}_{AB} = \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B \cong \mathbb{C}^4 \]

General \(n\)-qubit space: \(\mathcal{H}^{\otimes n} \cong \mathbb{C}^{2^n}\).

Key idea: \(n\) qubits have \(2^n\) basis states \(|x_1 x_2 \cdots x_n\rangle\), not \(2n\).

Prerequisite: Tensors, Qubits.

2) Product states (separable states)

A product state factorises across subsystems: \[ |\psi\rangle_{AB} = |\phi\rangle_A \otimes |\chi\rangle_B \] Example: \(|00\rangle = |0\rangle \otimes |0\rangle\), \(|+\rangle|0\rangle\).

Applying \(U \otimes V\) to a product state gives another product state — no entanglement is created without two-qubit gates.

Basis: \(\{|00\rangle, |01\rangle, |10\rangle, |11\rangle\}\) for two qubits.

3) Entangled states

A state is entangled if it cannot be written as a product. The Bell state: \[ |\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle + |11\rangle) \] is maximally entangled.

All four Bell states: \[ |\Phi^\pm\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle \pm |11\rangle),\quad |\Psi^\pm\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|01\rangle \pm |10\rangle) \]

Test: For \(|\psi\rangle = a|00\rangle + b|01\rangle + c|10\rangle + d|11\rangle\), separability requires \(ad = bc\).

Deeper treatment: Entanglement and Bell Inequalities.

4) Tensor product of operators

If \(U\) acts on qubit \(A\) and \(V\) on qubit \(B\), the joint operator is \(U \otimes V\): \[ (U \otimes V)(|\phi\rangle \otimes |\chi\rangle) = (U|\phi\rangle) \otimes (V|\chi\rangle) \]

CNOT is not a tensor product — it couples subsystems: \[ \mathrm{CNOT} = |0\rangle\langle 0| \otimes I + |1\rangle\langle 1| \otimes X \]

Gate reference: Quantum Gates, Quantum Circuits.

5) Partial trace and reduced states

Tracing out subsystem \(B\) from a density matrix \(\rho_{AB}\): \[ \rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB}) = \sum_i (\mathbb{I}_A \otimes \langle i|_B)\,\rho_{AB}\,(\mathbb{I}_A \otimes |i\rangle_B) \]

For \(|\Phi^+\rangle\): \(\rho_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(|\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|) = \frac{I}{2}\) — maximally mixed. Bob's half of a Bell pair alone carries no information.

Mixed states: Reduced states of entangled pure states are generally mixed. See Density Matrices.

6) Schmidt decomposition

Any pure bipartite state admits Schmidt decomposition: \[ |\psi\rangle_{AB} = \sum_i \sqrt{\lambda_i}\,|i\rangle_A \otimes |i\rangle_B \] with \(\lambda_i \geq 0\), \(\sum \lambda_i = 1\). The Schmidt rank is 1 iff the state is separable.

Schmidt coefficients measure entanglement: \(|\Phi^+\rangle\) has rank 2 with \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = \frac{1}{2}\).

7) Multi-qubit measurement

Measuring all qubits in the computational basis projects onto one of \(2^n\) outcomes with probability \(|\langle x_1\cdots x_n | \psi \rangle|^2\).

Measuring one qubit: Apply projectors \(P_0 = |0\rangle\langle 0| \otimes I\), \(P_1 = |1\rangle\langle 1| \otimes I\) — may not factor the post-measurement state if entangled.

Correlation: Bell states give perfectly correlated outcomes when both qubits are measured in \(Z\).

Measurement in Quantum Computing.

  1. How many basis states does a 4-qubit system have? What is the Hilbert space dimension?
  2. Is \(\frac{1}{2}(|00\rangle + |01\rangle + |10\rangle + |11\rangle)\) separable?
  3. Compute \(\mathrm{Tr}_B(|\Psi^-\rangle\langle\Psi^-|)\).
  4. Write CNOT as a sum of tensor products.
  5. What is the Schmidt rank of \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}|00\rangle + \sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}|11\rangle\)?
  6. Why does entanglement require interaction (two-qubit gates)?
