Hilbert Spaces

Postgraduate guide. Inner product spaces, orthogonality, Riesz representation, orthonormal bases, and spectral theory.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Inner product spaces

An inner product space \((H, \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle)\) is a vector space over \(\mathbb{R}\) or \(\mathbb{C}\) with a sesquilinear form satisfying positive definiteness: \[ \langle x, x \rangle \geq 0,\quad \langle x, x \rangle = 0 \Leftrightarrow x = 0 \]

Induced norm: \(\|x\| = \sqrt{\langle x, x \rangle}\) satisfies the parallelogram law \(\|x+y\|^2 + \|x-y\|^2 = 2(\|x\|^2 + \|y\|^2)\).
Cauchy–Schwarz: \(|\langle x, y \rangle| \leq \|x\|\,\|y\|\), with equality iff \(x, y\) are linearly dependent.

Prerequisite: Vector Spaces, Banach Spaces.

2) Hilbert spaces and completeness

A Hilbert space is a complete inner product space. Completeness is with respect to the norm induced by the inner product.

Examples:
  • \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(\mathbb{C}^n\) with the standard dot product
  • \(L^2(X, \mu)\) with \(\langle f, g \rangle = \int fg\,d\mu\) — see Measure Theory
  • \(\ell^2\) — square-summable sequences
Characterisation: A Banach space is a Hilbert space iff its norm satisfies the parallelogram law. Not every Banach space admits an inner product inducing its norm.

3) Orthogonality and projections

Vectors \(x, y\) are orthogonal (\(x \perp y\)) if \(\langle x, y \rangle = 0\). For a closed subspace \(M \subseteq H\), every \(x \in H\) decomposes uniquely as \(x = m + n\) with \(m \in M\) and \(n \in M^\perp\).

Projection theorem: The nearest point in a closed convex set \(C\) to \(x\) exists and is unique. For subspaces, the projection \(P_M x\) minimises \(\|x - m\|\) over \(m \in M\).
Best approximation: Orthogonal projection underpins least-squares fitting, Fourier series truncation, and the Galerkin method for PDEs.

4) Riesz representation theorem

For a Hilbert space \(H\), every bounded linear functional \(\varphi \in H^*\) has a unique representer \(y \in H\) such that \[ \varphi(x) = \langle x, y \rangle \quad \text{for all } x \in H \] with \(\|\varphi\| = \|y\|\).

Consequence: The map \(H \to H^*\), \(y \mapsto \langle \cdot, y \rangle\), is a conjugate-linear isometry. Hilbert spaces are self-dual (anti-isometrically).
Lax–Milgram lemma: For a coercive bilinear form \(a(u,v)\) on a Hilbert space, the variational problem \(a(u,v) = \langle f, v \rangle\) has a unique solution — the foundation of FEM.

5) Orthonormal bases

A set \(\{e_i\}_{i \in I}\) is an orthonormal basis (ONB) if \(\langle e_i, e_j \rangle = \delta_{ij}\) and the closed linear span is all of \(H\).

Parseval's identity: If \(\{e_i\}\) is an ONB, then for all \(x \in H\): \[ x = \sum_i \langle x, e_i \rangle e_i, \qquad \|x\|^2 = \sum_i |\langle x, e_i \rangle|^2 \]
Separable Hilbert spaces: A Hilbert space is separable iff it has a countable ONB. All infinite-dimensional separable Hilbert spaces are isometrically isomorphic to \(\ell^2\).
Fourier series: \(\{e^{inx/\ell}\}\) is an ONB of \(L^2([- \ell, \ell])\). See Fourier Series.

6) Spectral theory for bounded operators

For a bounded linear operator \(T: H \to H\), the spectrum is \(\sigma(T) = \{\lambda : T - \lambda I \text{ not invertible}\}\).

Self-adjoint operators: If \(T = T^*\) (with respect to the inner product), then \(\sigma(T) \subseteq \mathbb{R}\) and the spectral theorem provides functional calculus via projection-valued measures.
Compact self-adjoint: A compact self-adjoint operator has a discrete spectrum accumulating only at 0, with an ONB of eigenvectors — the infinite-dimensional spectral theorem.
Quantum mechanics: Observables are self-adjoint operators on a Hilbert space; measurement outcomes are eigenvalues.

7) Applications in PDE, signal processing, and control

Partial differential equations

Weak formulations in Sobolev spaces (Hilbert subspaces of distributions) use Riesz and Lax–Milgram for existence and uniqueness.

Signal processing

\(L^2\) inner products measure energy and correlation. Orthonormal bases (Fourier, wavelets) provide optimal approximations.

Related: Fourier Series, Control Theory, Measure Theory.
