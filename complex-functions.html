Complex Functions

Postgraduate guide. Holomorphic and analytic functions, Cauchy–Riemann equations, contour integration, residues, and conformal mapping.

← Pure Math Subjects Catalog

1) Complex differentiability and holomorphicity

A function \(f : D \subseteq \mathbb{C} \to \mathbb{C}\) is complex differentiable at \(z_0\) if the limit \[ f'(z_0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(z_0 + h) - f(z_0)}{h} \] exists (as \(h \in \mathbb{C}\) approaches 0 from any direction). If \(f\) is differentiable on an open set \(D\), it is holomorphic (synonymous with analytic on \(D\) — locally equal to its Taylor series).

Key idea: Complex differentiability is far stronger than real differentiability of \(u(x,y) + iv(x,y)\). A single limit from all directions forces rigid algebraic and geometric constraints.

Prerequisite: Complex Numbers and Differentiation.

2) Cauchy–Riemann equations

Write \(z = x + iy\) and \(f(z) = u(x,y) + iv(x,y)\). If \(f\) is holomorphic, then: \[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \qquad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \] If \(u, v\) have continuous first partials and satisfy C–R on \(D\), then \(f\) is holomorphic on \(D\).

Worked example: \(f(z) = z^2 = (x^2 - y^2) + i(2xy)\). Then \(u_x = 2x = v_y\) and \(u_y = -2y = -v_x\). Holomorphic on \(\mathbb{C}\).
Non-example: \(f(z) = \bar{z}\). Here \(u = x\), \(v = -y\), so \(u_x = 1\) but \(v_y = -1\). Not holomorphic anywhere.
Convention: \(\bar{z}\) is never holomorphic; conjugation breaks complex linearity of the differential.

3) Cauchy's theorem and integral formula

If \(f\) is holomorphic inside and on a simple closed contour \(\gamma\), then: \[ \oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 0 \] Cauchy's integral formula: for \(z_0\) inside \(\gamma\), \[ f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{z - z_0}\,dz \] Holomorphic functions are infinitely differentiable; Taylor coefficients are given by contour integrals.

Worked example: \(\oint_{|z|=2} \dfrac{1}{z^2 + 1}\,dz\). Poles at \(z = \pm i\) lie inside. By residue calculus (below): \(\dfrac{1}{2i} - \dfrac{1}{-2i} = \pi i\) (verify with partial fractions).

Related: Integration for contour techniques.

4) Laurent series and residues

Near an isolated singularity \(z_0\), \(f\) has a Laurent expansion \(f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n (z-z_0)^n\). The residue at \(z_0\) is \(a_{-1}\), and \[ \mathrm{Res}(f, z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{|z-z_0|=r} f(z)\,dz \] Residue theorem: \(\oint_\gamma f\,dz = 2\pi i \sum \mathrm{Res}(f, z_k)\) over poles inside \(\gamma\).

Worked example: \(\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{1+x^4}\,dx\). Close contour in upper half-plane; poles at \(e^{i\pi/4}\), \(e^{3i\pi/4}\). Residue sum gives \(\displaystyle \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}}\).
Simple pole formula: If \(f = g/h\) with \(g(z_0) \neq 0\), \(h(z_0) = 0\), \(h'(z_0) \neq 0\), then \(\mathrm{Res}(f, z_0) = g(z_0)/h'(z_0)\).

5) Conformal mappings and Möbius transformations

Holomorphic \(f\) with \(f'(z_0) \neq 0\) is conformal at \(z_0\): it preserves angles and orientation locally. Möbius (fractional linear) maps \[ f(z) = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}, \quad ad - bc \neq 0 \] are conformal on \(\mathbb{C} \cup \{\infty\}\) and map circles/lines to circles/lines.

Worked example: The Cayley map \(z \mapsto (z-i)/(z+i)\) sends the upper half-plane \(\mathbb{H}\) conformally onto the unit disk \(\mathbb{D}\) — central to Non Euclidean Geometry.
Schwarz–Christoffel: Maps the upper half-plane to polygonal regions — used in potential theory and fluid flow around corners.

6) Applications in engineering and physics

Signal processing

Analytic signals, Hilbert transforms, and filter design use holomorphic extensions to the upper half-plane (Kramers–Kronig relations).

Fluid dynamics

2D irrotational incompressible flow: velocity potential and stream function form a holomorphic complex potential \(W(z)\).

Electrostatics

Harmonic conjugates: electrostatic potential \(u\) and field lines from holomorphic \(\log z\), \(\arcsin z\) mappings in 2D.

Control theory

Nyquist criterion: stability from contour integrals of meromorphic transfer functions around encircled poles/zeros.

Related: Laplace Transforms, Fourier Series.

7) Advanced topics and research outlook

Beyond the undergraduate core: Riemann surfaces (multi-valued functions such as \(\sqrt{z}\), \(\log z\)), entire functions (Weierstrass factorisation, Picard's theorem), and several complex variables (Hartogs phenomena, domains of holomorphy).

Riemann mapping theorem: Every simply connected proper subdomain of \(\mathbb{C}\) is conformally equivalent to the unit disk. Uniqueness holds up to Möbius automorphisms of \(\mathbb{D}\).
Research context: Complex dynamics (Julia and Fatou sets), modular forms, and conformal field theory connect complex analysis to Chaos Theory and modern physics.
