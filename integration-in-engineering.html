üîå Integration in Electrical Engineering

1. Capacitors and Inductors

The behavior of capacitors and inductors involves time integrals:
\( V_C(t) = \frac{1}{C} \int i(t)\,dt + V_0 \)
\( i_L(t) = \frac{1}{L} \int v(t)\,dt + i_0 \)

2. Energy Calculations

Electrical energy is obtained by integrating power: \( E = \int P(t)\,dt \)
Capacitor and inductor stored energy: \[ E_C = \frac{1}{2}CV^2, \quad E_L = \frac{1}{2}LI^2 \]

3. Signal Processing

  • Op-amp integrators: \( V_{out} = -\frac{1}{RC} \int V_{in}(t)\,dt \)
  • Filtering and smoothing signals

4. Control Systems

PID controllers use integration for the integral control term: \[ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t)\,dt + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} \]

5. Electromagnetics

  • Gauss‚Äôs Law: \[ \oint_S \vec{D} \cdot d\vec{A} = Q_{\text{enclosed}} \]
  • Faraday‚Äôs Law: \[ \oint_C \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} \]

6. System Simulation

Simulation tools solve integral-differential equations such as: \[ L \frac{d^2i}{dt^2} + R \frac{di}{dt} + \frac{1}{C} i(t) = V(t) \]

7. Embedded Systems

  • Velocity from acceleration
  • Distance from velocity

8. Power Systems

Utility meters calculate energy as: \[ E = \int_0^T P(t)\,dt \]

‚úàÔ∏è Integration in Aircraft Engineering and Flight

1. Flight Dynamics and Navigation

Calculate velocity and position: \[ v(t) = \int a(t)\,dt, \quad s(t) = \int v(t)\,dt \] Essential in inertial navigation systems (INS).

2. Aerodynamic Forces

Total lift and drag from pressure: \[ L = \int_A p(x, y)\,dA \]

3. Structural Analysis

Beam deflection from moment: \[ y(x) = \int \int \frac{M(x)}{EI}\,dx^2 \]

4. Fuel and Range Estimation

Total fuel used: \[ F = \int_0^t \dot{F}(t)\,dt \]

5. Flight Control Systems

Same PID control structure as in electrical systems: \[ u(t) = K_p e(t) + K_i \int e(t)\,dt + K_d \frac{de(t)}{dt} \]

6. Propulsion and Thermodynamics

Mass flow and energy: \[ \dot{m} = \int_A \rho v\,dA \]

7. Vibration Analysis

Integration of angular velocity: \[ x(t) = \int \omega(t)\,dt \]

8. Flight Simulation

Solving nonlinear equations of motion with numerical integration (e.g., Runge-Kutta).
