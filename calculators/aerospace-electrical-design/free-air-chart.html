Single copper wire in free air

AC 43.13-1B Figures 11-4a and 11-4b, plotted from the same formula used in the Power Wire Analysis calculator.

How free-air ampacity is calculated

The FAA publishes two log-log charts for single unbundled copper wire in still air. Figure 11-4a covers smaller gauges (AWG 26–10; about 4–100 A). Figure 11-4b covers larger gauges (AWG 8 through 4/0; about 40–1,000 A). Each diagonal line is one wire size.

Reading the chart (graphical method)

  1. Compute the temperature difference ΔT = TR − T1 (conductor rating minus ambient).
  2. Find ΔT on the vertical axis (log scale).
  3. Move horizontally to the line for your AWG size.
  4. Read down to the horizontal axis for maximum free-air current in amperes.

That current is the x factor in the Power Wire Analysis grid (“De-rating — max conductor current in free air”). It is then multiplied by bundle and altitude factors to obtain Imax.

Formula used on this site

On log-log paper the lines are straight and parallel, which corresponds to a power law. Each gauge has a tabulated reference current Iref at ΔTref = 210 °C (the ELA default: TR = 260 °C, T1 = 50 °C). The calculator scales that reference when you change T1 or TR.

Max free-air current I = Iref × (ΔT / 210)1/2.0

Equivalent chart curve (for plotting) ΔT = 210 × (I / Iref)2.0

What the symbols mean

I — circuit or allowable current (A)
Current flowing in the wire, or the maximum allowable current read from the chart.
ΔT — temperature difference (°C)
TR − T1. On the chart this is “wire rating minus ambient”.
Iref — tabulated free-air current at ΔT = 210 °C
Fixed per AWG from AC 43.13 / ELA Table (see table below). Not a user input.
2.0 — log-log slope (exponent b)
Curve-fit to Fig 11-4; AC 43.13 lines are approximately parallel with b ≈ 1.8–2.0 on log-log paper.

Worked example

AWG 22, T1 = 50 °C, TR = 260 °C:
ΔT = 210 °C, Iref = 21 A → I = 21 × (210/210)1/2 = 21 A (on the AWG 22 line at ΔT = 210 on Fig 11-4a).

Same wire, T1 = 70 °C, TR = 260 °C:
ΔT = 190 °C → I = 21 × (190/210)0.5 ≈ 20.0 A (read a lower current for the same gauge because less temperature rise is available).

Relation to Imax in the calculator

Free-air current is only the first de-rating factor. The grid computes:

Imax = Ifree air × fbundle × faltitude

See the bundle chart (Fig 11-5) and altitude chart (Fig 11-6) for the other two factors.

Accuracy note

Digitized chart points and the ELA tabulated values do not lie on a single perfect power law; this implementation matches the ELA reference table at ΔT = 210 °C and follows the AC 43.13 log-log slope elsewhere. For contract or certification work, confirm against the official AC 43.13-1B charts.
