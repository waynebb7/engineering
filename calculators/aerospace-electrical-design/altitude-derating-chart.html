Altitude derating curve

AC 43.13-1B Figure 11-6, plotted from the same formula used in the Power Wire Analysis calculator.

How the factor is calculated

This page and the Power Wire Analysis calculator use a curve-fit of AC 43.13-1B Figure 11-6. The FAA chart is read graphically; this equation reproduces the curve to about ±0.006.

Current derating factor = 0.7 + 0.3 × (1 − h/100)1.5

What the result means

The factor is a multiplier between 0.7 and 1.0. Multiply it by a wire’s free-air current rating (after any bundle de-rating) to account for altitude. A factor of 1.0 means no altitude reduction (sea level); lower values mean more derating (less allowable current at high altitude).

Inputs you choose

h — altitude (×1,000 feet)
Aircraft altitude in thousands of feet, from 0 to 100 on the chart (0 to 100,000 ft). Enter altitude in feet in the calculator; divide by 1,000 to get h.

Limits (not user inputs)

Below sea level or at 0 ft: factor = 1.0. At or above 100,000 ft: factor = 0.7 (chart floor). Result is clamped to [0.7, 1.0].

Worked example

35,000 ft: h = 35
0.7 + 0.3 × (1 − 35/100)1.5 ≈ 0.857 (matches the calculator at 35,000 ft).
