Standards Reference

Engineering guidance and references for the Power Wire Analysis calculator. No calculations are performed on this page.

Calculator Standards reference Help

SAE ARP4404

Purpose: Aircraft wire current carrying capacity and conductor temperature calculation.

Used for: T2 calculations, current ratings, wire sizing.

Does NOT define: Human safe temperatures, cabin temperature limits, or installation acceptance limits.

AS50881

Purpose: Aircraft wiring installation practices.

Used for: EWIS routing, wire separation, support, clamping, environmental considerations, maintenance considerations.

Does NOT define: Fixed safe temperatures.

FAA AC 43.13-1B Chapter 11

Purpose: Aircraft electrical system installation guidance.

Used for: Wire routing, bundle derating, installation practices, protection methods.

Does NOT define: Fixed installation temperature limits.

FAA SFAR 88

Purpose: Fuel tank ignition prevention.

Used for: Fuel tank safety, ignition source prevention, wire routing in fuel zones.

Focus: Ignition prevention rather than conductor temperature.

FAA AC 25.981

Purpose: Fuel tank ignition prevention guidance.

Used for: Transport aircraft fuel system safety.

Focus: Elimination of ignition sources.

EWIS Requirements

Purpose: Electrical Wiring Interconnection System safety.

Used for: System safety assessment, fire prevention, routing requirements, maintenance requirements.

Focus: Preventing hazardous failures.

Human Factors Guidance

Human contact temperatures are generally assessed using human factors and occupational safety guidance rather than aircraft wire standards.

Temperature Typical engineering guidance
< 45 °CGenerally safe for prolonged contact
50 °C to 60 °CDiscomfort possible
60 °C to 70 °CPotential burn risk during prolonged contact
> 70 °CPotential burn hazard

These values are engineering guidance only and are not certification limits.

The calculator determines conductor temperature using SAE ARP4404 methodology. Installation acceptance criteria are project-specific and shall be established by the Design Authority using applicable certification requirements, system safety assessments, EWIS requirements, environmental requirements and approved aircraft design data.
